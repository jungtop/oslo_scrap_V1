71st akṣaya: Śamatha

71st Imperishable: Peaceful Meditation.


de la źi gnas mi zad pa gaṅ źe na?

tatra katamā śamathākṣayatā?

What then is the imperishability of peaceful meditation?

bsdus pa’i rnal ’byor la źugs pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ ni gaṅ sbyor ba daṅ mthoṅ ba daṅ bsgom pa’i lam na źi gnas rab tu sbyor ba ste, de’i dbaṅ du byas nas de la źi gnas mi zad pa gaṅ źe na źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so – "As for the bodhisattva progressing in the practice of collecting (saṃkṣepayogapratipanna), he is the one practising peaceful meditation on the way of practice, vision and cultivation (yaḥ prayogadarśanabhāvanāmārgeṣu śamathaprayuktaḥ); with reference to him (tam ādhipatiṃ kṛtvā) it is said: ...
źi gnas mi zad pa yaṅ źi gnas kyi tshogs mi zad pa daṅ źi gnas ñid mi zad pa ste, rnam pa gñis su rig par bya’o, de la gaṅ sems źi ba daṅ źes bya ba nas brtsams te btaṅ sñoms sgom źes bya ba yan chad ni źi gnas kyi tshogs so, bsam gtan daṅ po źes bya ba nas brtsams te dgu po ’di dag ni źi gnas źes bya’o źes bya ba yan chad kyis źi gnas ñid bstan to – "As for the imperishability of peaceful meditation (śamathākṣayatā): both the accumulations for peaceful meditation (śamathasaṃbhāra) and peaceful meditation itself (śamatha eva) are imperishable (akṣaya) ..."


gaṅ sems źi ba daṅ, rab tu źi ba daṅ, ñe bar źi ba daṅ,rnam par mi g-yeṅ ba daṅ, dbaṅ po legs par bsdams pa daṅ,mi rgod pa daṅ, mi ’phyar ba daṅ,mu cor mi smra ba daṅ,yab yab po ma yin pa daṅ,mi ’gyur ba daṅ,źi des pa daṅ,bsruṅs pa daṅ,caṅ śes pa daṅ,las su ruṅ ba daṅ,rtse gcig pa daṅ,gcig pur dga’ ba daṅ,’du ’dzi yoṅs su spoṅ ba daṅ,dben par dga’ ba daṅ,lus dben pa daṅ,sems ma bslad pa daṅ,dgon par mṅon du phyogs pa yid la byed pa daṅ,’dod pa ñuṅ ba daṅ,’dod pa med pa daṅ,’dod chen med pa daṅ,chog śes pa daṅ,’tsho ba yoṅs su dag pa daṅ,cho ga phun sum tshogs pa daṅ,spyod lam bsruṅs pa daṅ,dus śes pa daṅ,tshod śes pa daṅ,dus min śes pa daṅ,drod śes pa daṅ,dgaṅ sla ba daṅ,gso sla ba daṅ,so sor brtags pa’i stobs kyis mi mtho mi dma’ ba daṅ,ṅan du brjod pa daṅ ṅan du smras pa’i tshig gi lam daṅ du len pa daṅ.

yā cittasya śāntiḥ praśantir upaśāntiḥ;avikṣepakendriyasamyamaḥ;anuddhatatā anunnahanatā;amukharatā;acapalatā;acañcalatā;saumyatā,guptatā,ājāneyatā,karmaṇyatā,ekāgratā,ekāramatā,saṃgaṇikāvivarjanatā,vivekaratiḥ,kāyavivekaś,cittāvibhramaḥ,araṇyamukhamanasikāratā,alpecchatā,anabhilāṣatā,abahvicchatā,saṃtuṣṭiḥ,ājīvapariśuddhiḥ,cāritrasaṃpannatā,īryāpathaguptiḥ,kālajñatā,velājñatā,asamayajñatā,mātrajñatā,subhāratā,supoṣatā,pratisaṃkhyānabalenānunnāmāvanāmanatā,duruktadurāgatavacanapathādhivāsanatā.

[Accumulations for peaceful meditation (śamathasaṃbhāra): 1. The secondary accumulation (aprādhānyasaṃbhāra):][Inner] peace, [outer] calm and [inner and outer] tranquillity of thought [with no sloth and torpor (styānamiddha) ];[since it is peace, as there is not much inner thought-construction (vikalpa) ] no disturbance and [since it is calm as there are no thought-constructions of outer things] control of the senses;[since it is free of thought-constructions of both inner and outer things] neither haughtiness nor pride;no noisy speech [like that of kings and thieves];no unsteadiness [since it is not in the power of vices (klesavaśa) ];no wavering [as it cannot be disturbed by the adversaries of concentration (samādhivipakṣa) ];mildness [yielding to others],guarding oneself [since one vows not to enter the objects of the eyes and the other senses (cakṣurādîndriyaviṣaya) ],being well trained [or docility],fitness [being free of sloth and torpor (styānamiddha) ],one-pointedness,being content with being alone,avoiding crowds,happiness at being detached [feeling joy in one-pointed thought (cittaikāgrya) ],being detached in body [content with being alone and avoiding crowds],no fluctuation of thought,being mentally turned towards the wilderness [in the mountains and in caves (giriśailaguhā) ],having few wishes [for clothing (cīvara) and almsbowls (piṇḍapāta) ],not desiring [other things],not desiring much,contentment,purity of livelihood,perfection in behaviour,guarding one’s deportment,knowing the right time,knowing the proper moment,knowing the improper situation,knowledge of the proper quantity,moderation in eating,being easily fed,through careful consideration not being elated or depressed [when praised and reproached],enduring offensive and insulting ways of speech.

źi gnas kyi tshogs rnam pa gñis te, gtso bor gyur pa ma yin pa’i tshogs daṅ gtso ba’i tshogs so. gtso bor gyur pa ma yin pa’i tshogs kyi dbaṅ du byas nas sems źi ba daṅ źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so.naṅ du sems rnam par rtog pa mi maṅ ba ni sems źi ba’o, phyi rol gyi yul rnams la rtog par mi dga’ ba ni rab tu źi ba’o, phyi naṅ gñis la rtog pa med pa’am gñid daṅ rmugs pa med pa ni ñe bar źi ba’o, naṅ du sems rnam par rtog pa mi maṅ ba ste źi bas na rnam par mi g-yeṅ ba’o, phyi rol tu rtog pa med de rab tu źi bas na dbaṅ po legs par bsdams pa’o, phyi naṅ gñi gar rtog pa med pas na mi rgod pa daṅ mi ’phyar ba’o, rgyal po daṅ rkun po la sogs pa’i gtam maṅ po la mi ’jug pas na mu cor mi smra ba’o, ñon moṅs pa’i dbaṅ du mi ’gyur bas na yab yab po ma yin pa’o, tiṅ ṅe ’dzin gyi mi mthun pa’i phyogs kyis bskyod par mi nus pas na mi ’gyur ba’o.gźan gyi rjes su ’jug ciṅ źe sdaṅ ba’i sems med pas na źi des so; mig la sogs pa’i dbaṅ po’i yul la mi ’jug par sdom pas na bsruṅs pa’o; ñon moṅs pa spoṅ ba’i lam la źugs pas na caṅ śes pa’o; gñid daṅ rmugs pa med pas na las su ruṅ ba’o.dmigs pa’i yul la sems gźan du mi ’gro bas na rtse gcig pa’o; gcig pur gnas par mos pas na mos pa’o; ’gro ba maṅ po daṅ lhan cig mi gnas pas na ’du ’dzi yoṅs su spoṅ ba’o; sems rtse gcig pa la mos pa’i phyir dben par dga’ ba’o; gcig pur dga’ źiṅ ’du ’dzi spoṅ bas na lus dben pa’o; ñon moṅs pas sems dkrugs pa med pas na sems ma bslad pa’o; ri daṅ brag phug la sogs par gnas par sems mṅon par ’du byed pas na dgon par mṅon du phyogs par yid la byed pa’o.chos gos daṅ lhuṅ bzed la sogs pa ñi tshe tsam ’chaṅ bas na ’dod pa ñuṅ ba’o; de las lhag par mi ’dod pas na ’dod pa med pa’o; ṅan pas chog par ’dzin pas na ’dod chen med pa’o; bzaṅ po mi tshol bas na chog śes pa’o.log pa’i ’tsho ba med pas na ’tsho ba yoṅs su dag pa’o; tshul khrims rnam par dag pas na cho ga phun sum tshogs pa’o; spyod lam thams cad du ñon moṅs pa mi skye źiṅ spyod lam mdzes pas spyod lam bsruṅs pa’o.dus daṅ tshod daṅ dus min śes pa ni sṅar bśad pa bźin du lta’o; kha zas maṅ du yaṅ mi za, ñuṅ du yaṅ mi za ste, ran par byed pas na drod śes pa’o; cuṅ zad tsam gyis chog śes par ’dzin pas na dgaṅ sla ba daṅ gso sla ba’o.so sor brtags pa’i stobs kyis mi mtho mi dma’ źes bya ba ni tiṅ ṅe ’dzin daṅ ldan pa’i śes rab kyi mthus chos rnams ji yaṅ ma yin par rtogs pa’i phyir bstod pa la sogs pas mtho bar yaṅ mi ’gyur, smad pa la sogs pas dma’ bar yaṅ mi ’gyur ba’o; ṅan du brjod pa ni sṅon ’das pa’i dus su’o, ṅan du smras pa ni da ltar gyi dus ñid na’o.


rnal ’byor la mṅon du phyogs pa’i sems bskyed pa daṅ,naṅ du yaṅ dag ’jog la mṅon par dga’ ba daṅ,bsam gtan gyi yan lag yid la byed pa daṅ,byams pa bskyed pa daṅ,sñiṅ rje mṅon par sgrub pa daṅ,dga’ ba la gźog pa daṅ,btaṅ sñoms sgom pa daṅ.

yogābhimukhacittotpādaḥ,pratisaṃlayanābhiratiḥ,dhyānāṅgamanasikāratā,maitryutpādaḥ,karuṇābhinirhāraḥ,muditāsthāpanā,upekṣābhāvanā.

[The primary accumulation (prādhānyasaṃbhāra):]Producing thoughts bent on religious practice [that is producing thoughts for cultivating peaceful meditation and expanded vision (śamathavipaśyanābhāvanācittotpāda):]taking pleasure in retiring for meditation,mental effort with the limbs of meditation as the object,producing friendliness,and engendering compassion,[the two being roots of concentration (samādhi) ], establishing [one’s thoughts] in joy,[producing undisturbed concentration (akopyasamādhi), and thus] cultivating equanimity.

gtso bo’i tshogs bstan pa’i phyir rnal ’byor la mṅon du phyogs pa’i sems bskyed pa źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so. Cf. ṭ. fol. 257a7.źi gnas daṅ lhag mthoṅ bsgom pa’i sems bskyed pa ni rnal ’byor la mṅon du phyogs pa’i sems bskyed pa’o; yaṅ dag ’jog la mṅon par dga’ źes bya ba ni de ñid kyi tiṅ ṅe ’dzin bsgom pa la mos pa’o; bsam gtan gyi yan lag yid la byed pa daṅ źes bya ba ni bsam gtan la sogs pa’i yan lag rtog dpyod daṅ bcas pa daṅ rtog dpyod med pa la sogs pa bsgom pa sṅa nas yid la byed ciṅ rtsom pa’o; byams pa bskyed pa daṅ sñiṅ rje mṅon par bsgrub pa źes bya bas ni tiṅ ṅe ’dzin gyi rtsa bar gyur pa tshad med pa gñis sṅa nas skyed ciṅ sgrub par byed pa’o; dga’ ba la gźog ces bya ba la sogs pa ni tiṅ ṅe ’dzin mi ’khrugs par byed pa dga’ ba tshad med pa la sems gźog ciṅ btaṅ sñoms tshad med pa bsgom la, de gñis kyi sems daṅ rtag par byed pa’o.


bsam gtan daṅ po daṅ, bsam gtan gñis pa daṅ, bsam gtan gsum pa daṅ, bsam gtan bźi pa daṅ, nam mkha’ mtha’ yas skye mched daṅ, rnam śes mtha’ yas skye mched daṅ, ci yaṅ med pa’i skye mched daṅ, ’du śes med ’du śes med min skye mched daṅ, ’du śes daṅ tshor ba ’gog pa mthar gyis sñoms par ’jug pa dgu po ’di dag ni źi gnas śes bya’o.

prathamadhyānaṃ dvitīyadhyānaṃ tṛtīyadhyānaṃ caturthadhyānam ākāśānantyāyatanaṃ vijñānānantyāyatanam akiṃcanyāyatanaṃ naivasaṃjñānāsaṃjñāyatanaṃ saṃjñaveditanirodha imā navānupūrvasamāpattaya ucyate śamathaḥ.

[Peaceful meditation itself is:]The first [state of] meditation, the second meditation, the third meditation, the fourth meditation, the field of [meditation where the] infinity of space [is experienced], the field of infinity of consciousness, the field of not anything in particular, the field of neither concept nor no concept, the cessation of concept and feeling, (p. 147) these nine successive states of meditation are called peaceful meditation.

źi gnas bstan pa’i phyir bsam gtan daṅ po daṅ źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs te, mthar gyis sñoms par ’jug pa ’di dag gi don ni sṅar bśad par zad do. Cf. supra ṭ. fol. 257a5-6.


’di ltar źi gnas kyi tshogs ni tshad med pa ste; źi gnas kyi tshogs de dag la gaṅ brtson pa, ’di ni źi gnas mi zad pa źes bya’o.

evaṃ śamathasaṃbhārā aprameyāḥ; yas teṣu śamathasaṃbhāreṣv udyogo ’yam ucyate ’kṣayaḥ śamathaḥ.

Thus the accumulations for peaceful meditation are immeasurable [as the moments of existence which are causes of cultivating peaceful meditation (śamathabhāvanāhetu) are immeasurable like space (yathākāśa) ], and thus exertion in those accumulations of peaceful meditation is called imperishable peaceful meditation [since it produces immeasurable (apramāṇa) peaceful meditation].

źi gnas kyi tshogs ni tshad med pa ste źes bya ba la sogs pa ni źi gnas bsgom pa’i rgyu’i chos nam mkha’ daṅ ’dra bar tshad med de, źi gnas kyi tshogs de dag la gaṅ brtson pa rnams kyaṅ źi gnas tshad med par skye bar ’gyur bas na źi gnas mi zad pa źes bya’o źes gsuṅ so.
