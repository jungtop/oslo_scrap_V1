18th aksaya: Pūrvanivāsānusmṛtiḥ

18th Imperishable: Remembrance of Former Lives.(p. 102)


de la byaṅ chub sems dpa’i sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran pa’i śes pa mṅon sum du byed pa’i mṅon par śes pa gaṅ źe na?

tatra katamā bodhisattvasya pūrvanivāsānusmṛtijñānasākṣātkaraṇābhijñā?

What then is the bodhisattvas’ supernormal knowledge which consists in realizing the knowledge of the remembrance of former lives?

bsam pa mṅon sum pa bstan pa’i phyir de la byaṅ chub sems dpa’i sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran pa’i śes pa mṅon sum du byed pa’i mṅon par śes pa gaṅ źe na źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so – "To teach what [the bodhisattva] facing intentions (āśayābhimukha) is ...".
de mi zad pa yaṅ: sṅon gyi dus ’di tshun chad dran no, źes dran pa’i mtha’ thug pa med pa ni mi zad pa’am, yaṅ na bdag gi sṅon gnas dran pa ’ba’ źig ma yin gyi, sems can thams cad kyi sṅon gyi gnas kyaṅ dran pas sems can mi zad pa’i phyir de dag gi sṅon gyi gnas dran pa yaṅ mi zad pa’o. rnam pa gcig tu dran pa de ni chos kyi dbyiṅs kyi gnas kyis byin gyis brlabs pa’o źes bya bas kyaṅ mi zad pa bstan te, chos kyi dbyiṅs rnam par dag pa ni mtshan ma daṅ dṅos po thams cad las ’das pas mi zad pa yin te, des byin gyis brlabs pa yaṅ de daṅ mthun pa’am, de’i raṅ bźin rtog pas, de lta bu’i dran pa yaṅ mtha’ med pa’i phyir mi zad pa’o. de yaṅ rnam pa bźir rig par bya ste, 1) gaṅ gis dran pa daṅ, 2) gaṅ dran pa daṅ, 3) ji ltar dran pa daṅ, 4) dran nas don chen por ’gyur ba’o – "It is imperishable (akṣaya): ’One remembers earlier times up till now’, thus the limit (anta) of remembrance is not found (anavastha) and thus it is imperishable (akṣaya). Or, again, as one remembers the earlier lives of all beings and not only those of oneself, since the beings are imperishable (akṣaya), remembering their earlier lives is also imperishable (akṣaya). In short, also by "That remembrance is sustained by the sphere of all moments of existence (sā smṛtiḥ svadhiṣṭhitā dharmadhātusthityā)" which is taught [infra], it is imperishable (akṣaya): the pure sphere of all moments of existence (viśuddhadharmadhātu) transcends all distinguishing marks and existing things (sarvanimittabhāvātikrānta) and is thus imperishhable (akṣaya); to be supported by that [sphere] is to be in accordance with it, or, to discern its essence (svabhāva), and thus that remembrance (smṛti) is imperishable (akṣaya) as it is endless (ananta). This should be known through four forms: ... 1) de dran pa des sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran te źes bya ba nas brtsams te, pha rol tu phyin pa thams cad kyis tshogs bsags pa’o źes bya ba yan chad kyis gaṅ gis dran pa bstan to, 2) de dran pa des sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran te źes bya ba nas brtsams te, bskal pa brgya stoṅ phrag du ma yaṅ dran źes bya ba yan chad kyis gaṅ dran pa bstan to, 3) sems can miṅ che ge mo źes bya ba nas brtsams te, des bdag daṅ sems can gźan gyi sṅon gyi mtha’ nas bzuṅ ste, sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran źes bya ba yan chad kyis ji ltar dran pa bstan to, 4) de bdag gi dge ba’i rtsa ba źes bya ba nas brtsams te, thams cad mkhyen pa’i phyir bsṅo ste źes bya ba yan chad kyis don chen por ’gyur ba bstan to.


de dran pa des sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran te.dran pa de ni chos kyi dbyiṅs kyi gnas kyis śin tu byin gyis brlabs pa’o.rab tu rig pas na, mi g-yo ba’o;ye śes śin tu yoṅs su sbyaṅ ba byas pas na, mi rgod pa’o;źi gnas la rab tu gnas pas na, ’tshe ba med pa’o;lhag mthoṅ gis śin tu zin pas na, nor ba med pa’o;mṅon sum du śes pas na, gźan gyi driṅ la mi ’jog pa’o;dran źiṅ mi brjed pa’i chos can pas na, dran par bya ba’o;theg pa chen po rab tu śes pa’i phyir, bsod nams kyi tshogs bsags pa’o;gźan la rag ma lus pas na ye śes kyi tshogs bsags pa’o;thams cad kyi pha rol tu ’gro ba’i phyir, pha rol tu phyin pa thams cad kyi tshogs bsags pa’o.

so ’nayā smṛtinā pūrvanivāsān anusmarati;sā smṛtiḥ svadhiṣṭhitā dharmadhātusthityā,acalitā pravedanatvāt,anuddhatā sukṛtajñānaparikarmatvāt,nirupadravā śamathapratiṣṭhitatvāt,abhrāntā vipaśyanāsuparigṛhītatvāt,aparapraṇeyā pratyakṣajñānatvāt,smartavyā smṛtyasaṃpramoṣadharmatvāt,puṇyasaṃbhāropacayā mahāyānaparijñānatayā,jñānasaṃbhāropacayāparādhīnatvāt,sarvapāramitāsaṃbhāropacayā sarvapāramiṃgatatayā.

[1. By what he remembers (yena smarati):] He remembers his former lives with that remembrance;that remembrance is sustained by [that is, born from as a cause (hetu), or has the same essence (svabhāva) as] the sphere of all moments of existence,it is steadfast through [correctly (aviparīta) ] making known [all moments of existence],not agitated since it is well prepared through knowledge,beyond harm since it is established in peaceful meditation,beyond deception through attaining expanded vision [that is, insight (prajñā) ],not dependent on any other because of immediate knowledge,to be remembered through having the quality of recollection and not forgetting,the accumulation of merit through knowing the great way,the accumulation of knowledge since it is not contingent on anything else [in thriply purified (trimaṇḍalapariśuddha) meditation (dhyāna) ],the accumulation of all the perfections through transcending everything.

de źes bya ba ni byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de źes bya ba’i tha tshig go, des źes bya ba ni sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran pa des źes bya ba’i don to.dran pa de ni chos kyi dbyiṅs kyi gnas kyis byin gyis brlab pa’o źes bya ba la gzuṅ ba daṅ ’dzin pa thams cad las ’das pa’i chos kyi dbyiṅs ñid saṅs rgyas kyi chos thams cad skye ba’i rgyu yin pas chos kyi dbyiṅs kyi gnas te, dran pa de’i chos kyi gnas de’i rgyu las skyes pa’am, chos kyi dbyiṅs daṅ raṅ bźin mthun pa’i phyir byin gyis brlabs pa źes bya’o.rab tu rig pas na mi g-yo ba’o źes bya ba ni dran pa des chos thams cad phyin ci ma log par śin tu rtog pa na ’khrul pa’am ’gyur ba med ces bya ba’i don to.ye śes śin tu sbyaṅ ba byas pas na mi rgod pa’o źes bya ba ni śes śiṅ dran par byas pa’i dṅos po la rab tu byaṅ bar gyur pas na ’phyar ba daṅ rgod pa med ces bya ba’i tha tshig go.źi gnas la rab tu gnas pas na gtse ba med ces bya ba ni tiṅ ṅe ’dzin sems rtse gcig pa’i mtshan ñid la legs par ’dug pas tiṅ ṅe ’dzin daṅ mi mthun pa’i phyogs phyin ci log tu rtog ciṅ dpyod pa la sogs pa’i skyon med ces bya ba’i don to.lhag mthoṅ gis śin tu zin pas na nor ba med ces bya ba la lhag mthoṅ ni śes rab la bya ste, dran par bya ba’i dṅos po rnams śes rab kyis ma nor bar zin ciṅ rtog pas na ma ’khrul pa’o.
This item is omitted in ṭ.dran źiṅ mi brjed pa’i chos can pas na źes bya ba la sṅon thos pa’i chos daṅ don rnams ma brjed pa ni dran pa’o, phyis brjed par mi ’gyur ba ni mi brjed pa ste, de lta bu’i chos daṅ ldan pas sṅon gyi gnas dran pa’i mṅon par śes pa dran par bya’o.theg pa chen po rab tu śes pa’i phyir źes bya ba ni dran pa des theg pa chen po’i chos khoṅ du chud pa’i phyir khoṅ du chud pa’i rgyu las byuṅ ba’i bsod nams kyi tshogs daṅ ldan źes bya ba’i don to.gźan la rag ma lus pas na ye śes kyi tshogs bsags pa’o źes bya ba ni bsam gtan ’khor gsum yoṅs su dag pa gźan gyi driṅ la mi ’jog pas ye śes kyi tshogs bsags pa’o.thams cad kyi pha rol tu ’gro ba’i phyir źes bya ba la pha rol ces bya ba ni mthar phyin pa’am mchog tu phyin pa la bya ste, pha rol tu phyin pa thams cad kyi mchog gam, mthar phyin pa’i phyir pha rol tu phyin pa thams cad kyi tshogs bsags pa’o.


de dran pa des sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran te, des tshe rabs gcig kyaṅ rjes su dran no, gñis daṅ, gsum daṅ, bźi daṅ, lṅa daṅ, bcu daṅ, ñi śu daṅ, sum cu daṅ, bźi bcu daṅ, lṅa bcu daṅ, tshe rabs brgya yaṅ rjes su dran no, tshe rabs stoṅ daṅ, tshe rabs brgya stoṅ daṅ, tshe rab brgya phrag du ma daṅ, tshe rabs stoṅ phrag du ma daṅ, tshe rabs brgya stoṅ phrag du ma daṅ, chags pa’i bskal pa daṅ, ’jig pa’i bskal pa daṅ, chags pa’i bskal pa du ma daṅ, ’jig pa’i bskal pa du ma daṅ, chags pa daṅ ’jig pa’i bskal pa du ma yaṅ rjes su dran no, bskal pa brgya daṅ, bskal pa stoṅ daṅ, bskal pa brgya stoṅ daṅ, bskal pa brgya phrag du ma daṅ, bskal pa stoṅ phrag du ma daṅ, bskal pa brgya stoṅ phrag du ma yaṅ rjes su dran te:

so anayā smṛtinā pūrvanivāsān anusmarati. sa ekajātim apy anusmarati, dve tisraś catasraḥ pañca daśa viṃśatis triṃśac catvāriṃśat pañcāśaj jātiśatam apy anusmarati, jātisahasraṃ jātiśatasahasrāṇy anekāni jātiśatāny anekāni jātisahasrāṇy anekāni jātiśatasahasrāṇi, vivartakalpaṃ saṃvartakalpam anekān vivartakalpān anekān saṃvartakalpān anekān vivartasaṃvartakalpān apy anusmarati, kalpaśataṃ kalpasahasram kalpaśatasahasrāny anekāni kalpaśatāny anekāni kalpasahasrāny anekāni kalpaśatasahasrāṇy apy anusmarati:

[2. What he remembers (yat smarati):] He remembers his former lives with that remembrance, he remembers one birth, two, three, four, five, ten, twenty, thirty, forty, fifty, he remembers even a hundred births, a thousand births, a hundred thousand births, several hundred births, several thousand births, several hundred thousand births, a world-age of manifestation, a world-age of destruction, several world-ages of manifestation, several world-ages of destruction, he remembers even several world-ages of manifestation and destruction, a hundred world-ages, a thousand world-ages, a hundred thousand world-ages, several hundred world-ages, several thousand world-ages, he remembers even several hundred thousand world-ages:

tshe rabs gcig kyaṅ dran no źes bya ba ni skye ba gcig kyaṅ dran no źes bya ba’i tha tshig go. gźan ni go sla bas ma bśad de, gaṅ dran pa yaṅ bśad zin to.


sems can miṅ che ge mo źes bya ba de dag byuṅ bar gyur pa, der bdag kyaṅ byuṅ ste, bdag gi miṅ ni ’di źes bya ba, rigs ni ’di źes bya ba, rus ni ’di źes bya ba, kha dog ni ’di ’dra ba, kha zas ni ’di lta bu źig za za, tshe’i tshad ni ’di tsam źig thub thub, yun ni ’di srid cig gnas gnas, bde ba daṅ sdug bsṅal ni ’di lta bu źig myoṅ ṅo. bdag de nas śi ’phos nas, che ge mo źig tu skyes so; de nas kyaṅ śi ’phos nas, ’dir skyes te, de ltar rnam pa daṅ bcas, rgyu mtshan daṅ bcas, yul phyogs daṅ bcas par sṅon gyi gnas rnam pa maṅ po rjes su dran no.

amukās te sattvā āsann evaṃnāmānas, tatrāham apy āsam evaṃ nāmaivaṃjātir evaṃgotra evaṃvarṇa evamāhāra evamāyuṣparyanta evaṃcirasthitika evaṃsukhaduḥkhapratisaṃvedī, ahaṃ tataś cyuto ’mutropapannas, tataś cyuta ihāpy upapanna iti.

[3. How he remembers (yathā smarati):] “Those beings there had such and such a name, there I also existed with such a name, in such a social class, in such a family, with such a complexion, eating such food, having such a long life, staying for so long a time, experiencing such pleasure and pain, dying there, being born yonder, dying there, being born here.”

bdag gi miṅ ni ’di źes bya ba la sogs pa la tshe rabs gaṅ du skyes pa’i tshe re re’i miṅ yaṅ dran pa ste, dper na tshe ’di’i tshe na ni miṅ yaṅ ’di źes bya, tshe ’di’i tshe na ni miṅ ’di źes bya ba dran pa’o; rigs ’di źes bya ba ni tshe rabs de dag tu skyes pas na bdag rigs bźi las gaṅ daṅ gaṅ du skyes pa dran pa’o; rus źes bya ba ni rigs bźi las kyaṅ mthon dman du phye ba śes pas te, tshe rabs de dag na bdag gi pha’i rus ni ’di źes bya, ma’i rus ni ’di źes bya źes bya ba la sogs pa’o; kha dog ni ’di ’dra źes bya ba ni kha dog yid du ’oṅ ba daṅ, yid du mi ’oṅ ba daṅ, mdzes pa daṅ mi mdzes pa ci ’dra bar gyur pa daṅ dran pa’o; kha zas ni ’di lta bu źig za za źes bya ba ni ston pa dran pa ste, tshe rabs de daṅ de dag tu kha zas ci daṅ ji lta bu zos pa dran pa’o; tshe’i tshad ni ’di tsam źig thub ces bya ba ni tshe’i ’du byed dran pa ste, tshe rabs de dag tu tshe’i tshad ji srid gnas gnas pa las tshe zad de ni lan du śi, ye ’drog gis ni lan du bsad pa dran pa’o; bde ba daṅ sdug bsṅal myoṅ ba źes bya bas ni dar ba daṅ rgud pa dran pa bstan te, tshe rabs de daṅ de dag tu dar ciṅ bde ba lan maṅ du myoṅ ba daṅ, rgud ciṅ sdug bsṅal ba lan maṅ du myoṅ ba so sor dran pa’o; ji ltar bdag gis dran pa de bźin du sems can gźan gyis yaṅ dran par sbyar ro.


des bdag daṅ sems can gźan gyi sṅon gyi mtha’ nas bzuṅ ste sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran no.

sākārān sānimittān soddeśān anekavividhapūrvanivāsān anusmarati, sa ātmaparasattvapūrvāntaprabhṛti pūrvanivāsān anusmarati.

Thus he remembers different former lives with their forms, characteristics and places. He remembers his own as well as (p. 103) other beings’ former lives from the beginning.

rnam pa daṅ bcas źes bya ba la rnam pa ni tshe rabs gcig nas bskal pa bye ba khrag khrig maṅ po dran pa’o; rgyu mtshan ni dus daṅ rus la sogs pa gaṅ yin pa dran pa’o; yul phyogs ni bdag de nas śi ’phos nas che ge mo źig tu skyes źes dran pa’o. ji ltar dran pa bśad zin to.


de bdag gi dge ba’i rtsa ba daṅ, sems can gźan gyi dge ba’i rtsa ba sṅon gyi rgyu las byuṅ ba dran źiṅ; bdag gi dge ba’i rtsa ba dran nas, byaṅ chub tu yoṅs su sṅo bar byed do, sems can gźan gyi dge ba’i rtsa ba dran nas, sems can rnams byaṅ chub tu sems bskyed du ’dzud do.gaṅ sṅon gyi ’du byed kyi sdug bsṅal de yaṅ mi rtag pa daṅ, sdug bsṅal ba daṅ, stoṅ pa ñid daṅ, bdag med par rton par byed do.de mi rtag pa daṅ, sdug bsṅal ba daṅ, stoṅ pa ñid daṅ, bdag med par rton pas gzugs kyis dregs pa med pa yin, kha dog gis dregs pa med pa yin, nad med pas dregs pa med pa yin, laṅ tshos dregs pa med pa yin, ’tsho bas dregs pa med pa yin, loṅs spyod kyis dregs pa med pa yin, g-yog ’khor gyis dregs pa med pa yin, dbaṅ phyug gis dregs pa med pa yin,brgya byin du ’dod pa med pa yin, tshaṅs par ’dod pa med pa yin, ’jig rten skyoṅ bar ’dod pa med pa yin, ’khor los sgyur bar ’dod pa med pa yin, skye ba thams cad ’dod pa med pa yin no.’dod pa daṅ rgyal srid kyi dbaṅ phyug la dbaṅ byed pa thams cad kyaṅ bdag la bde ba’i phyir mi ’dod de; gźan du na, sems can thams cad yoṅs su smin par bya ba’i phyir bsams bźin du srid par skye ba ’dzin to.de mi rtag pa daṅ, sdug bsṅal ba daṅ, stoṅ pa ñid daṅ, bdag med par rton pas sṅon gyi kun nas ñon moṅs pa’i spyod pas ṅo tsha źiṅ ’dzem ste, gnod źiṅ rnam par smod do.da ltar gyi tshe yaṅ srog gi phyir yaṅ mi bya ba mi byed ciṅ, de sṅon gyi dge ba’i rtsa ba rnams byaṅ chub tu yoṅs su bsṅo ste, rgyas par byed do.da ltar byuṅ ba’i dge ba’i rtsa ba yaṅ sems can thams cad daṅ thun moṅ du yoṅs su bsṅo’o; des mi ’thun par yoṅs su sṅo ba spaṅs te; saṅs rgyas kyi rigs daṅ, chos kyi rigs daṅ, dge ’dun gyi rigs rgyun mi ’chad pa daṅ, thams cad mkhyen pa’i phyir yoṅs su bsṅo ste;

sa svakuśalamūlāni parasattvakuśalamūlāni pūrvahetukāni smarati, svakuśalamūlāni smṛtvā bodhaye pariṇāmayati, parasattvakuśalamūlāni smṛtvā ca sattvān bodhicittotpāde ’vatārayati.yat pūrvasaṃskāraduḥkhaṃ tasya tv anityatāṃ duḥkhaṃ śūnyatāṃ nairātmyaṃ pratisarati.sa nānityatāduḥkhaśūnyatānairātmyapratisaraṇena rūpagarvito na varṇagarvito nārogyagarvito na yauvanagarvito nājīvagarvito na bhogagarvito na parijanagarvito naiśvaryagarvitaḥ,śakrānarthiko brahmānarthiko lokapālānarthikaś cakravartyanarthikaḥ sarvajātyanarthikaḥ.sarvakāmarājyaiśvaryādhipatyam api nātmasukhāyārthayate, anyathā sarvasattvaparipācanāya saṃcintyabhavopapattiṃ parigṛhṇāti.so ’nityaduḥkhaśūnyanairātmyapratisaraṇena pūrvasaṃkliṣṭacaryāyā lajjati jihreti nigṛhṇāti vigarhate.pratyutpanne ’py adhvani jīvitahetor apy akaraṇīyaṃ na karoti, sa pūrvakuśalamūlāni bodhaye pariṇāmayati bṛṃhayati.pratyutpannāny api kuśalamūlāni sarvasattvebhyaḥ sādhāraṇāni pariṇāmayati, sa pratikūlapariṇāmanaṃ parityajati, buddhavaṃsadharmavaṃsasaṃghavaṃsānupacchedāya sarvajñānāya ca pariṇāmayati.

[4. The great purpose when he has remembered (smṛtvā mahârthikatā):] He remembers his own roots of good and the roots of good of other beings with their causes in the past, and having remembered his own roots of good he transforms them into awakening, having remembered the roots of good of other beings he inspires them to produce the thought of awakening.He relies on the impermanence, suffering, emptiness and egolessness in suffering caused by past formative factors.He does not, however, get conceited because of his reliance on impermanence, suffering, emptiness and selflessness, nor conceited by complexion, nor conceited by health, nor conceited by youth, nor conceited by his way of living, nor conceited by his pleasures, nor conceited by followers, nor conceited by lordliness,not seeking to be the king of the gods, nor seeking to be the lord of the world, nor seeking to be a protector of the world, nor seeking to be a universal king, nor wishing any kind of birth.He does not seek pleasures, kingship, lordship or power for his own pleasure, quite the opposite, he seeks birth in existence intentionally for the sake of maturing living beings.By reliance on impermanence, suffering, emptiness and selflessness he feels ashamed of his former impure actions, he rejects and despises them.In the present he does not do anything which ought not to be done even for the sake of his life, and he transforms and expands the former roots of good for the sake of awakening.He transforms the roots of good in the present for the sake of all beings collectively, he gives up transformation contrary to this [namely to be born as a disciple, an isolated buddha, a god or a man] and transforms them for the sake of the continuity of the lineage of the Buddhas, the lineage of religion and the lineage of the community, and for the sake of omniscience.

sṅon gyi rgyu las byuṅ ba dran źes bya ba ni dge ba’i rtsa ba sṅon ci daṅ ci bsags pa dran pa’o; byaṅ chub tu yoṅs su sṅo bar byed do źes bya ba ni dge ba’i rtsa ba’i rgyu yod par dran źiṅ śes nas dge ba’i rtsa ba de dag byaṅ chub tu bsṅo bar byed pa’o.sṅon gyi ’du byed kyi sdug bsṅal źes bya ba ni bdag sṅon ’gyur ba’i sdug bsṅal gyis sdug bsṅal bar gyur pa’am, ’du byed kyis sdug bsṅal bar gyur pa de dag dran nas źes bya ba’i tha tshig go; rton par byed do źes bya ba ni ’du byed rnams mi rtag pa la sogs par lta’o źes bya ba’i don to.gzugs kyis dregs pa med pa yin źes bya ba ni de ltar mi rtag par śes pas bdag ni gzugs bzaṅ po mdzes pa’o sñam pa’i rlom sems med pa ste, mdzes pa de yaṅ de ma thag tu med par ’gyur bar khoṅ du chud pa’i phyir te, kha dog gis dregs pa med pa la sogs pa la yaṅ ci rigs par sbyar ro.skye ba thams cad ’dod pa ma yin źes bya ba ni skye ba’i gnas thams cad du skye bar ’dod par mi skye źes bya ba’i don to.bsams bźin du srid par skye ba ’dzin źes bya ba ni sems can gyi don du śes bźin du khams gsum du skye bar byed pa’osṅon gyi kun nas ñon moṅs pa’i spyod pas ṅo tsha źiṅ ’dzem ste źes bya ba ni chos thams cad mi rtag pa’i mtshan ñid du śes pas, de lta bu dag gi phyir sṅon sdig pa byas pa ni mi rigs pa byas so źes ṅo tsha źiṅ ’dzem pa skye ba’o.rgyas par byed do źes bya ba ni dge ba’i rtsa ba bsags pa byaṅ chub tu bsṅos nas ’phel źiṅ rgya chen por byed pa’o.des mi mthun par yoṅs su bsṅo ba spaṅs te źes bya ba ni dge ba’i rtsa ba ci bsags pa daṅ, ñan thos sam, raṅ saṅs rgyas sam, lha’am, mir skye ba mi bsṅo’i, dkon mchog gsum gyi rigs rgyun mi chad pa daṅ, thams cad mkhyen pa’i ye śes thob par bya ba’i phyir bsṅo’o. ’di’i don chen por ’gyur ro.


’di ni byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran pa śes pa mṅon sum du byed pa’i mṅon par śes pa mi zad pa’o.

iyam ucyate bodhisattvānām akṣayā pūrvanivāsānusmṛtijñānasākṣātkaraṇābhijñā.

This is called the bodhisattvas’ supernormal knowledge which consists in realizing the knowledge of the remembrance of former lives.

sṅon gyi gnas rjes su dran pa śes pa mṅon du byed pa’i mṅon par śes pa mi zad pa bśad zin to.
