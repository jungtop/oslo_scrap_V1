15th aksaya: Divyacakṣuḥ

15th Imperishable: Divine Sight.


de la byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi lha’i mig gaṅ źe na?

tatra katamad bodhisattvānāṃ divyacakṣuḥ?

What then is the bodhisattvas’ divine sight?

de la gzugs mṅon sum bstan pa’i phyir byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi lha’i mig gaṅ źe na źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so. gzugs mṅon sum pa de yaṅ rnam pa bźir rig par bya ste, 1) ’jig rten daṅ ’jig rten las ’das pa las khyad źugs pa daṅ, 2) gzugs thams cad mthoṅ ba daṅ, 3) mi mthun pa’i dri mas ma gos pa daṅ, 4) rgyu rnam par dag pa’o; de la 1) gaṅ lha thams cad kyi lha’i mig ces bya ba nas brtsams te, chos ’od gsal te źes bya ba yan chad kyis ’jig rten daṅ ’jig rten las ’das pa thams cad las khyad źugs pa bstan to, 2) lha’i mig des phyogs bcu’i ’jig rten gyi khams źes bya ba nas brtsams te, de dag thams cad yoṅs su rdzogs par byed do źes bya ba yan chad kyis gzugs thams cad mthoṅ ba bstan to, 3) de dag gi mig de ni gzugs thams cad mthoṅ bas źes bya ba nas brtsams te, lam la ma chags par spyod pa’o źes bya ba yan chad kyis mi mthun pa’i dri mas ma gos par bstan to, 4) de ci’i phyir źe na źes bya ba nas brtsams te, mṅon par śes pa ma ñams źes bya ba yan chad kyis rgyu rnam par dag pa bstan to – "To teach what the bodhisattva facing forms (rūpābhimukha) is ... "


gaṅ lha thams cad kyi lha’i mig daṅ, gaṅ klu thams cad daṅ, gnod sbyin thams cad daṅ, dri za daṅ, lha ma yin daṅ, nam mkha’ ldiṅ daṅ, mi’am ci daṅ, lto ’phye chen po daṅ, mi daṅ mi ma yin pa thams cad kyi lha’i mig daṅ, gaṅ slob pa daṅ mi slob pa’i ñan thos thams cad kyi lha’i mig daṅ, gaṅ raṅ saṅs rgyas thams cad kyi lha’i mig, de dag pas byaṅ chub sems dpa’ thams cad mkhyen pa ñid las ṅes par byuṅ ba rgya chen po las ṅes par byuṅ ba rnams kyi lha’i mig ni ches mchog go, ches dam pa’o, ches rab bo, ches phul lo, ches gsal lo, ches bzaṅ ṅo, ches stobs daṅ ldan no, ches ’od gsal te.

yat sarvadevānāṃ divyacakṣur, yat sarvanāgānāṃ, sarvarakṣasāṃ, sarvagandharvāsuragaruḍakiṃnaramahoragamānuṣyāmanuṣyāṇāṃ divyacakṣur, yat sarvaśaikṣāśaikṣāṇāṃ śrāvakānāṃ divyacakṣur, yat sarvapratyekabuddānāṃ divyacakṣuḥ, sarvajñatāniryātānām udāraniryātānāṃ bodhisattvānāṃ divyacakṣus tebhyo ’grataraṃ parataraṃ varataraṃ pravarataraṃ spaṣṭataraṃ bhadrataraṃ balavattaraṃ prabhāsvarataram.

[1. Distinguished both as worldly and as unworldly (lokalokottaraviśiṣṭa):]As for the divine sight of all the gods, the divine sight of all snake-gods and all demons, the divine sight of all celestial musicians, giants, divine birds, man-horses, great serpents, men and not men, the divine sight of all disciples with their training completed or not, the divine sight of all the isolated buddhas, compared with all these the divine sight of the bodhisattvas perfecting omniscience and perfecting the exalted [practices] is better, higher, more eminent, more excellent, clearer, finer, stronger and more brilliant.

byaṅ chub sems dpa’ thams cad mkhyen pa ñid las ṅes par byuṅ ba źes bya ba ni thams cad mkhyen pa’i ye śes thob par bya ba’i phyir bsṅos pas lha’i mig de lta bu thob bo źes bya ba’i don to – "... He attains that kind of divine sight (divyacakṣur) by transforming into the attainment of the knowledge of omniscience (sarvajñajñānaprāpaṇāya pariṇāmanayā), such is the meaning."
rgya chen po las ṅes par ’byuṅ ba rnams kyi lha’i mig ni ches mchog go źes bya ba la byaṅ chub sems dpa’i spyod pa pha rol tu phyin pa la sogs pa rgya chen po’am, lam gyi ye śes rgya chen po spyod pa las thos pa’i lha’i mig ni ches mchog go źes bya ba’i don to, ches mchog go ces bya ba ni lha daṅ klu la sogs pa ’jig rten pa ’phags pa ma yin pa rnams kyi lha’i mig pas ñan thos daṅ raṅ saṅs rgyas ’phags pa rnams kyi lha’i mig mchog go, ’phags pa de dag gi lha’i mig pas kyaṅ byaṅ chub sems dpa’i lha’i mig mchog pas na de ñid las bzla ba’i phyir ches mchog ces bya’o; ci’i phyir ches mchog ce na? ’jig rten pa rnams kyis zag pa daṅ bcas pa daṅ, ’phags pa ñan thos la sogs pa ’jig rten las ’das pa rnams kyi mṅon par śes pa yaṅ ’das pa daṅ, ma ’oṅs pa daṅ, dbaṅ pos lhag pa daṅ, khams kyis lhag pa la sogs pa goṅ ma rnams śes par mi nus la cuṅ zad śes śiṅ tshad yod kyi, byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi de lta ma yin te, tshad daṅ mtha’ med pa’i phyir ches mchog go, de la mchog ni bkra śis pa’i phyir ro; ches dam pa ni goṅ nas goṅ du khyad par du ’gro ba’i phyir ro; ches rab ni thams cad kyi thog mar ’gro ba’i phyir ro; ches phul ni gzugs thams cad la khyab pa’i phyir ro; ches gsal ba ni dri ma med ciṅ rab tu daṅ ba’i phyir ro; ches bzaṅ ba ni ma smad ciṅ mya ṅan las ’das pa daṅ mthun pa’i phyir ro; ches stobs daṅ ldan pa ni thogs pa med par gzugs kun mthoṅ ba’i mthu chen po daṅ ldan pa’i phyir ro; ches ’od gsal ba ni ye śes dri ma med pa’i mthu mchog daṅ ldan pa’i phyir ro. ’jig rten daṅ ’jig rten las ’das pa las khyad źugs pa bśad zin to.gzugs thams cad mthoṅ ba bstan pa’i phyir de lha’i mig des źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so.


lha’i mig des phyogs bcu’i ’jig rten gyi khams mtha’ yas mu med pa dag na gzugs su snaṅ ba rags pa’am, phra ba’am, bzaṅ po’am, ṅan pa’am, rgyaṅ riṅ po’am, thag ñe ba gaṅ ci yaṅ ruṅ ste, de dag thams cad de dag gi lha’i mig des rig ciṅ snaṅ la śin tu rtogs, śin tu rig, śin tu mthoṅ ste.

tena divyacakṣuṣā ye kecid anantāparyanteṣu daśadiglokadhātuṣu rūpapratibhāsāḥ sthūlā vā sūkṣmā vā praṇītā vā hīnā vā dūrā vā nikaṭā vā te sarve tena teṣāṃ divyacakṣuṣā viditā avabhāsitāḥ supratividdhāḥ suviditāḥ sudṛṣṭāḥ.

[2. Seeing all forms (sarvarūpadarśana):] By that divine sight, whatever manifestations of form occur in the endless and limitless worlds in the ten directions, be they coarse or subtle, superior or inferior, distant or near, all those are known, made manifest, penetrated, thoroughly known, thoroughly seen by that divine sight of theirs.

lha’i mig des śes bya ba’i goṅ du bśad pa ’jig rten daṅ ’jig rten las ’das pa’i lha’i mig thams cad las ’das par gyur pa’i lha’i mig des śes bya ba’i don to; gzugs su snaṅ ba źes bya ba ni gzugs su gtogs pa źes bya ba’i tha tshig go; rags pa’am źes bya ba ni chen por gtogs pa thams cad bsdus pa ste, gaṅs ri daṅ ri rab la sogs pa’o, phra mo źes bya bas chuṅ ṅur gtogs pa thams cad bsdus pa ste, rdul daṅ rdul phran la sogs pa’o, bzaṅ po’am źes bya bas bzaṅ po thams cad bsdus pa ste, gaṅ ’phags pa slob pa daṅ mi slob pa la sogs pa’i gzugs so, ṅan pa’am źes bya bas ṅan pa thams cad bsdus pa ste, gaṅ so so’i skye bo’i gzugs rnams so, rgyaṅ riṅ po’am źes bya bas rgyaṅ riṅ por gtogs pa thams cad bsdus pa ste, gaṅ ’jig rten gyi khams brgya stoṅ du ma’i pha rol na gnas pa’o, thag ñe ba gaṅ ci yaṅ ruṅ źes bya bas ñe ba thams cad bsdus pa ste, gaṅ thag ñe ba na ’dug pa’i gzugs thams cad do; yaṅ na rags pa ni ’byuṅ ba chen po bźi rnams so, phra ba ni ’byuṅ ba chen po bźi las gyur pa’i gzugs rnams so; yaṅ na rags pa ni ’jig rten pa’i gzugs rnams so, phra ba ni ’jig rten las ’das pa’i gzugs rnams so; yaṅ na rags pa ni ’dod pa’i khams su gtogs pa’i gzugs so, phra ba ni gzugs kyi khams su gtogs pa’i gzugs so; bzaṅ po ni bde ’gro rnams kyi gzugs so, ṅan pa ni ṅan soṅ ba rnams kyi gzugs so; yaṅ na bzaṅ po ni dbyibs daṅ kha dog tu ldan pa’o, ṅan pa ni dbyibs daṅ kha dog tu mi ldan pa’o; rgyaṅ riṅ po ni ’das pa daṅ ma ’oṅs pa’i gzugs so, thag ñe ba ni da ltar gyi gzugs so; yaṅ na rgyaṅ riṅ po ni gźan gyi rgyud du gtogs pa’i gzugs so, thag ñe ba ni bdag gi rgyud du gtogs pa’i gzugs so; de dag thams cad ni goṅ du bśad pa’i gzugs de dag thams cad do; de dag gi ni byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de dag gi lha’i mig gis so; rig ciṅ snaṅ ba la sogs pa ni mthoṅ ba’i rnam graṅs kyi tshig go; yaṅ na ’das pa’i gzugs mthoṅ ba ni rig pa’o, da ltar gyi gzugs mthoṅ ba ni snaṅ ba’o, ma ’oṅs pa’i gzugs mthoṅ ba ni śin tu rtogs pa’o, sbom po rnams mthoṅ ba ni śin tu mthoṅ ba’o; yaṅ na mos pa’i spyod pa’i tiṅ ṅe ’dzin drod kyi rgyu las byuṅ bas na rig pa’o, spyi bo’i rgyu las byuṅ bas na snaṅ ba’o, bzod pa’i rgyu las ’byuṅ bas na rtogs pa’o, ’jig rten pa’i chos kyi mchog gi rgyu las byuṅ bas na śin tu rig pa’o, mthoṅ ba’i lam gyi rgyu las byuṅ bas na śin tu mthoṅ ba’o.
Cf. Mvy 1256-59.


phyogs bcu’i ’jig rten gyi khams mtha’ yas mu med pa dag na sems can gzugs med pa ma gtogs pa’i sems can gaṅ dag ’gro ba thams cad du skyes pa, de dag thams cad kyi ’chi ’pho daṅ skye ba yaṅ dag pa ji lta ba bźin du rab tu śes so; de dag gi las kyi rgyu yaṅ yaṅ dag pa ji lta ba bźin du rab tu śes so; de dag gi las kyi ’bras bu daṅ dbaṅ po’i rim pa yaṅ yaṅ dag pa ji lta ba bźin du rab tu śes so.

ye sattvā anantāparyanteṣu daśadiglokadhātuṣv ārūpyasattvān varjayitvā sarvagatiṣv upapannās, teṣām sarveṣāṃ cyutyupapattīr yathābhūtaṃ prajānāti, teṣāṃ karmahetūn yathābhūtaṃ prajānāti, teṣāṃ karmaphalānīndriyavaimātratāṃ ca yathābhūtaṃ prajānāti.

All beings in the endless, limitless worlds in the ten directions (p. 95) – excluding the formless beings – born in any state of existence, he knows as it is the death and birth of all of them, he knows as it is the causes of their actions, he knows as it is the fruits of their actions as well as the diversity of their abilities.

’chi ’pho daṅ skye ba yaṅ dag pa ji lta ba bźin du rab tu śes so źes bya ba ni ’chi ba daṅ ’pho ba gñis kyaṅ phuṅ po ’dor źiṅ len pa la bya ste, gzugs kyi phuṅ po len ciṅ ’dor ba mig gi spyod yul yin pas lha’i mig gis śes par ba’o; de dag gi las kyi rgyu yaṅ dag pa ji lta ba bźin du rab tu śes so źes bya ba ni sems can de dag gi lus daṅ ṅag gi sgo nas ci spyod pa de’i rgyu bstun ji spyod spyod pa las byuṅ ba yaṅ rab tu śes so źes bya ba’i don to. las kyi ’bras bu śes pa ni da lta’i lus daṅ ṅag gi sgo nas las ci byas pa’i ’bras bu phyis cir ’gyur ba śes pa’o; dbaṅ po’i rim pa śes pa ni mig la sogs pa’i dbaṅ po rnams daṅ, dad pa la sogs pa’i dbaṅ po rno rtul gyi rim pa śes pa ste, las kyi rgyu daṅ ’bras bu daṅ dad pa la sogs pa’i dbaṅ po ’di dag gzugs kyi rgyu las byuṅ ba’i phyir lha’i mig gi spyod yul yin par śes par bya’o.


phyogs bcu’i ’jig rten gyi khams mtha’ yas mu med pa dag na saṅs rgyas bcom ldan ’das rnams kyi saṅs rgyas kyi źiṅ gi yon tan bkod pa gaṅ yin pa, de dag thams cad mthoṅ ste. mthoṅ nas kyaṅ raṅ gi tshul khrims yoṅs su dag pas raṅ gi źiṅ gi yon tan bkod pa yoṅs su dag par bya ba’i phyir bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyi tshogs bsags pa rnams yoṅs su bsṅo ste, tshul khrims kyi yon tan la gnas pa de dag gi yoṅs su bsṅo ba yoṅs su ’grub par ’gyur ro.

ye ’nantāparyanteṣu daśadiglokadhātuṣu buddhānāṃ bhagavatām buddhakṣetraguṇavyūhās, tān paśyati, dṛṣṭvā ca svaśīlapariśuddhyā svakṣetraguṇavyūhapariśuddhaya upacīyamānān puṇyajñānasaṃbhārān pariṇāmayati, teṣāṃ ca śīlaguṇeṣu sthitānāṃ pariṇāmānā pariniṣpadyate.

As for the splendour of qualities in the buddha-fields of the Awakened Lords in endless, limitless worlds in the ten directions, all of that he sees. Having seen it, he transforms by the purity of his own morality the accumulated multitudes of merit and knowledge into purification of the splendour of qualities in his own field. And the transformation performed by those established in the qualities of morality is thus accomplished.

saṅs rgyas kyi źiṅ gi yon tan bkod pa źes bya ba ni saṅs rgyas kyi źiṅ sa gźi bai dū rya’i kha dog daṅ dpag bsam gyi śiṅ gi phreṅ ba daṅ bdud rtsi’i rdziṅ la sogs pa daṅ ldan pa’o; raṅ gi tshul khrims yoṅs su dag pas raṅ gi źiṅ gi yon tan bkod pa yoṅs su dag par bya ba’i phyir bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyi tshogs bsags pa rnams yoṅs su sṅo ste źes bya ba ni saṅs rgyas bcom ldan ’das rnams kyi źiṅ gi yon tan mthoṅ nas bdag kyaṅ nam saṅs rgyas su ’gyur ba’i tshe źiṅ gi yon tan de lta bu daṅ ldan par gyur cig ces bdag gi bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyi tshogs ci bsags pa rnams tshul khrims yoṅs su dag pa’i mthus bsṅo źes bya ba’i don to, tshul khrims kyi mthus bsṅos pas ji bźin ’grub par ’gyur ram sñam pa la tshul khrims kyi yon tan la gnas pa de dag gi bsṅo ba yoṅs su ’grub par ’gyur ro źes bya ba smos te, chos ñid kyi tshul khrims daṅ ldan pa rnams ci bsams pa’am smon pa bźin du ’grub pas bsṅos pa bźin du yoṅs su ’grub ces bya ba’i don to.


de dag gi lha’i mig des saṅs rgyas bcom ldan ’das de dag gi byaṅ chub sems dpa’i dge ’dun gaṅ yin pa, de dag thams cad kyaṅ mthoṅ ṅo. mthoṅ nas kyaṅ skyes bu dam pa de dag gi cho ga daṅ, spyod pa daṅ, spyod yul daṅ, dran pa daṅ, śes bźin daṅ, spyod lam daṅ, gnas daṅ, mthu daṅ, chos daṅ, rnam par thar pa daṅ, ye śes daṅ, rton pa daṅ, gzuṅs daṅ, spobs pa la mkhas pa daṅ, śes rab daṅ, ye śes daṅ, thabs la mkhas pa la ’jug pa gaṅ yin pa, de dag thams cad yoṅs su rdzogs par byed do.

tena teṣām divyacakṣuṣā ya āmiṣāṃ bhagavatāṃ bodhisattvasaṃghās tān sarvāṃś ca paśyati, dṛṣṭvā ca yat teṣāṃ satpuruṣāṇām ācāracāritragocarasmṛtisaṃprajanyeryāpathasthānabaladharmavimokṣajñānapratisaraṇadhāraṇīpratibhānakauśalyaṃ yaś ca prajñājñānopāyakauśalyāvatāras tat sarvaṃ paripūrayati.

By the divine sight of theirs he also sees all the congregations of bodhisattvas belonging to those Lords. Having seen them, he fulfils the penetration into skill in insight, knowledge and expedient means, as well as the skill in right action, behaviour, where to go, recollection, awareness, conduct, where to live, powers, religion, liberation [the extinction established nowhere (apratiṣṭhitanirvāṇa) ], knowledge, way of confidence, remembrance and eloquence of those good men.

cho ga daṅ źes bya ba la bya ba daṅ mi bya ba rnams ni cho ga’o; chos gos bgo ba daṅ, lhuṅ bzed bzuṅ ba la sogs pa chos bźin du byed pa ni spyod pa’o; chaṅ ’tshoṅ daṅ smad ’tshoṅ la sogs pa spyod yul lṅar mi ’jug pa ni spyod yul lo, ltuṅ ba daṅ mi ltuṅ ba ma brjed pa ni dran pa’o, ltuṅ ba daṅ mi ltuṅ ba śes pa ni śes bźin no, ’gro ba daṅ, ’dug pa daṅ, ñal ba daṅ, ’chag pa la sogs pa mdzes pa ni spyod lam mo, gtsug lag khaṅ daṅ, ri daṅ, brag phug la sogs pa ni gnas so, mṅon par śes pa lṅa daṅ ldan pa daṅ bdud daṅ mu stegs can gyis mi byed pa ni mthu’o, byaṅ chub sems dpa’i mdo sde daṅ, ’dul ba daṅ, chos mṅon pa ni chos so, mi gnas pa’i mya ṅan las ’das pa ni rnam par thar pa’o, me loṅ lta bu’i daṅ, mñam pa ñid daṅ, so sor kun tu rtog pa daṅ, bya ba sgrub pa’i ye śes ni ye śes so, mkhas pa’i tshig re re daṅ yaṅ sbyar te, don daṅ, chos daṅ, ye śes daṅ, ṅes pa’i don gyi mdo sde la rton pa ni rton pa la mkhas pa’o, don daṅ mi brjed pa’i gzuṅs daṅ ldan pa ni gzuṅs la mkhas pa’o, ’khor ba’i mtha’i bar du chos bśad kyaṅ spobs pa zad par mi ’gyur ba ni spobs pa la mkhas pa’o, raṅ daṅ spyi’i mtshan ñid rnam par ’byed pa ni śes rab bo, chos thams cad stoṅ par khoṅ du chud pa ni ye śes so, dga’ ldan gyi pho braṅ nas ’chi ’pho ba la sogs pa ston pa ni thabs la mkhas pa la ’jug pa de dag thams cad yoṅs su rdzogs par byed do źes bya ba ni lha’i mig daṅ ldan pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ des ’jig rten gyi khams gźan gyi byaṅ chub sems dpa’i yon tan de dag mthoṅ nas bdag ñid kyis kyaṅ de dag thams cad yoṅs su rdzogs par byed do. gzugs thams cad mthoṅ ba bśad zin to.


de dag gi mig de ni gzugs thams cad mthoṅ bas na, sgrib pa med pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni gzugs thams cad la mi chags pas na, chags pa med pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni bag la ñal daṅ, lta ba daṅ, kun nas ldaṅ ba las rnam par grol ba’o;de dag gi mig de ni raṅ bźin gyis ’od gsal bas na, rnam par dag pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni yul thams cad la mi gnas pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni ñon moṅs pa daṅ bag la ñal thams cad mi skye ba’o;de dag gi mig de ni the tshom med pas na, rab rib med pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni sgrib par ’gyur ba’i chos thams cad la kun nas ma laṅs pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni chos thams cad snaṅ ba la gsal ba thob pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni ye śes la rton pa ste, rnam par śes pa’i spyod yul ma yin no;de dag gi mig de ni ñon moṅs pa thams cad kyis ma chags, mi sdaṅ, ma rmoṅs pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni ’phags pa’i dbaṅ po rnams la ṅes par ’byed pa’i cha daṅ ’thun pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni sems can thams cad la ’od zer mñam par gtoṅ bas na, thogs pa med pa’i ye śes ñid daṅ;de dag gi mig de ni sems kyi mi dge ba’i chos daṅ bral bas na, rnam par dag pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni raṅ bźin gyis kun nas ñon moṅs pa can ma yin pas na, dri ma med pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni bsam pa ma btaṅ bas na, saṅs rgyas kyi spyan gyis mṅon par bsgrubs pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni rjes su chags pa daṅ khoṅ khro ba daṅ bral ba’i phyir, ma chags ma bciṅs pa’o;de dag gi mig de ni don gyi spyod yul gyi bden pas ñe bar bsgrubs śiṅ chos rnam par dag pa śes pa’i lam la ma chags par spyod pa’o.

Not sullied by opposing impurities (pratikūlamalânupalipta, vipakṣânupalipta):] That sight of theirs is unhindered since it sees all forms;that sight of theirs is unattached since it is beyond cupidity;that sight of theirs is free from habits, viewpoints and the manifestations of vices;that sight of theirs is pure since it is originally luminous;that sight of theirs is not dependent on any field of perception;that sight of theirs is non-origination of habits and vices;that sight of theirs is free of blindness since it is beyond doubt;that sight of theirs does not arise among the obscuring moments of existence;that sight of theirs has attained clarity concerning the manifestations of all moments of existence;that sight of theirs relies on knowledge, not being within the range of consciousness;that sight of theirs is not impassioned, enraged or deluded by any vices;that sight of theirs is an aid to penetration into the abilities of the holy;that sight of theirs is unattached knowledge since it shines equally on all beings [that is, looking similarly on all with compassion (karuṇā) ];that sight of theirs is pure since it is freed from the bad moments of existence in thoughts;that sight of theirs is impeccable since it is essentially beyond impurity;that sight of theirs is created from the sight of the Buddhas since they never give up their intention [to reach omniscience];that sight of theirs is unattached and unborn since it is beyond aversion;that sight of theirs is produced by [having as the object] the truth pertaining to the sphere of [real] meaning, it is the unattached treading of the path of pure knowledge.

[4. The pure cause (viśuddhahetu):] Why [is the divine sight of those bodhisattvas as above explained]?Because those good men are established in great compassion,knowing the moments of existence,penetrating the meaning of things [namely their emptiness (śūnyatā) ],are faultless,they preach religion in accordance with what they have seen and heard,they are turned away from the bad,turned towards the place of awakening,their thoughts are not hindered in anything,they are generous to the stingy,having pity on the immoral,they are without violent thoughts towards the violent-minded,(p. 96) bringing vigour to the lazy,showing the methods of meditation to those who are not concentrated,giving the eye of insight to those with limited insight,showing the way to those on bad ways,making those of inferior conviction understand the exalted religion of the awakened,causing them to penetrate the knowledge [characterized by the absence of thought-constructions (nirvikalpalakṣaṇa) ] of the omniscient,they possess unhindered supernormal knowledge for the sake of fulfilling awakening.
