IX. Nirdeśabalaprāpti

IX. Attaining the Power of Teaching


btsun pa śa ra dva ti’i bu, gźan yaṅ byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi so so yaṅ dag par rig pa bźi mi zad pa ste. bźi gaṅ źe na? gaṅ ’di don so so yaṅ dag par rig pa daṅ; chos so so yaṅ dag par rig pa daṅ; ṅes pa’i tshig so so yaṅ dag par rig pa daṅ; spobs pa so so yaṅ dag par rig pa’o.

punar aparaṃ, bhadanta śāradvatīputra, bodhisattvānāṃ catasraḥ pratisaṃvido ’py akṣayāḥ. katamāś catasraḥ? yā imā arthapratisaṃvid dharmapratisaṃvin niruktipratisaṃvit pratibhānapratisaṃvit.24th Imperishable: Knowledge of The Meaning.

Further, reverend Śāradvatīputra, the bodhisattvas’ four kinds of knowledge are also imperishable. Which four? 1) Knowledge of the meaning, 2) knowledge of moments of existence, 3) knowledge of interpretation and 4) knowledge of eloquence.

ston par byed pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ bstan pa’i phyir btsun pa śa ra dva ti’i bu gźan yaṅ byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi so so yaṅ dag par rig pa bźi mi zad pa ste źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs te, de mi zad pa yaṅ byaṅ chub sems dpa’ so so yaṅ dag par rig par bya ba’i don daṅ chos la sogs pa la mtha’ thug pa med pa de mi zad pa’i phyir ro. ’dir yaṅ bstan pa’i tshul daṅ bśad pa’i tshul du rig par bya ste, bźi gaṅ źe na? gaṅ ’di don so so yaṅ dag par rig pa daṅ źes bya ba la sogs pas ni bstan pa’i tshul bstan pa’o, lhag ma rnams kyis ni bśad pa’i tshul bstan to. ston par byed pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnam pa bźi ste, 1) mtshan ñid śes pa daṅ, 2) ’du śes śes pa daṅ, 3) tha sñad śes pa daṅ, 4) brjod pa śes pa’o. so so yaṅ dag par rig pa bźis sems can rnams la ci dgar chos ston par nus pas de dag gis bsdus pa’i don bstan par bya ba’i phyir mthu thob pa bstan to – "To teach what the bodhisattva teaching (deśaka) is it is said ... . They are imperishable (akṣaya), since the limit (anta) of the things, moments of existence, etc., to be known by the bodhisattva is nowhere established (anavasthā) and they are thus imperishable (akṣaya). ..."
