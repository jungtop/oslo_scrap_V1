36th akṣaya: Cittasmṛtyupasthāna

36th Imperishable: The Presence of Recollection Concerned with Thought.(p. 130)


de la byaṅ chub sems dpa’i sems la sems kyi rjes su lta ba dran pa ñe bar gźag pa gaṅ źe na?gaṅ byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de’i byaṅ chub kyi sems ma brjed pa, dran pas bsruṅs śiṅ mi g-yeṅ ba ste,

tatra katamad bodhisattvasya citte cittānupaśyanā smṛtyupasthānam?yā tasya bodhisattvasya bodhicittāsaṃpramoṣatā smṛtyārakṣaṇatāvikṣiptatā.

What then is the bodhisattva’s presence of recollection which consists in the consideration of thoughtIt is not forgetting the thought of awakening, guarding it with recollection, and no lack of concentration.

rab tu gnas pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ gaṅ źe na źes gaṅ sems dran pa ñe bar gźag pa la rab tu źugs pa ste, rnam par śes pa’i gnas thams cad las ’das yaṅ thob la, byaṅ chub sems dpa’i tiṅ ṅe ’dzin tshad med pa yoṅs su rdzogs par byed pa’o. de’i dbaṅ du byas nas de la byaṅ chub sems dpa’i sems la sems kyi rjes su lta ba dran pa ñe bar gźag pa gaṅ źe na? źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs te, byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyis bsgom par bya ba ni byaṅ chub kyi sems so – "What is the established (pratiṣṭhita) bodhisattva? He is the one progressing in the presence of recollecting thoughts (cittasmṛtyupasthānapratipanna), who attains transcendence from all states of consciousness (vijñānasthiti) and accomplishes the immeasurable concentration of the bodhisattvas (apramāṇāṃ bodhisattvasamādhiṃ paripūrayati). With reference to him, it is said (tam adhipatiṃ kṛtvā) ... What is to be cultivated (bhāvayitavya) by the bodhisattvas is the thought of awakening (bodhicitta)".byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de’i byaṅ chub kyi sems ma brjed pa źes bya ba la sogs pa la ñan thos daṅ raṅ saṅs rgyas dag sems dran par ñe bar gźag pa na bdag gi sems mi rtag pa la sogs pa dran pa ñe bar ’jog pa lta bu ni ma yin gyi, byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams thog mar byaṅ chub tu sems ji ltar bskyed pa’i byaṅ chub kyi sems de mi brjed pa’i gzuṅs kyis dran pas mi brjed pa daṅ, dran pa ñid ma ñams par bsruṅ ba daṅ, bsruṅs pa ñid mi mthun pa’i phyogs la mi g-yeṅ bar byed pa ni sems dran pa ñe bar ’jog pa źes bya’o – "... It is not like when the disciples and isolated buddhas practice recollection concerned with the impermanence, etc., of one’s own thoughts, the bodhisattvas do not forget the original thought of awakening (ādibodhicitta) because of recollection (smṛti), because of unfailing memory (asaṃpramoṣadhāraṇī). ...


de sems la ’di ltar rtog go:sems ni skyes nas źig ciṅ źu ste mi gnas pa’o, naṅ na yaṅ mi ’dug, phyi rol tu yaṅ ma ’phos, gñi ga med par yaṅ mi dmigs te;gaṅ bdag gi byaṅ chub kyi sems daṅ po skyes pa, de ni zad ciṅ ’gags te yal źiṅ med par gyur pas yul na mi gnas, phyogs na mi gnas te, de ni gdags par mi nus so.sems gaṅ dag gis byaṅ chub kyi phyir dge ba’i rtsa ba rnams yaṅ dag par bsgrubs pa, de dag kyaṅ zad ciṅ ’gags te yal źiṅ med par gyur pas yul na mi gnas, phyogs na mi gnas te, de yaṅ gdags par mi nus so.sems gaṅ dag gis dge ba’i rtsa ba de dag byaṅ chub tu yoṅs su bsṅos pa, de dag kyaṅ de’i raṅ bźin daṅ de’i mtshan ñid de;sems kyis sems mi śes so, sems kyis sems mi mthoṅ ṅo, sems kyis sems kyi mtshams mi sbyor ro.

sa cittam evaṃ pratyavekṣate:cittam utpannaṃ bhagnavilīnam anavasthitam, nāpy adhyātmam avasthitaṃ nāpy bahirdhā cyutaṃ nobhayam antareṇopalabhyate.yan me prathamam utpannaṃ bodhicittaṃ tat kṣīṇaniruddhaṃ cyutāntarhitam adeśastham apradeśasthaṃ tan na prajñapituṃ śakyate.yaiś cittair bodhaye kuśalamūlāḥ samudānītās tāny api kṣīṇaniruddhāni cyutāntarhitāny adeśasthāny apradeśasthāni tāny api na prajñāpayituṃ śakyante.yaiś cittais te kuśalamūlā bodhaye pariṇāmitās tāny api tatsvabhāvāni tallakṣaṇāni.cittaṃ na cittena jñāyate, cittaṃ na cittena dṛśyate, cittaṃ na cittena pratisaṃdadhāti.

He looks at [the] thought [of awakening] this way:“Thought [that is, thought itself and the thought of awakening] never remains once it has arisen, it is broken up and melts away as unstable, it does not remain internally, it does not perish outwardly, it is not perceived in between the two.The first thought of awakening arisen in me has perished and ceased, vanished and disappeared, it is not found in any place, not in any location, it is not possible to communicate it.And the thoughts by which the roots of good are accumulated for the sake of awakening, these have also perished and ceased, vanished and disappeared, they are not found in any place, not in any location, it is not possible to communicate them.The thoughts by which roots of the good are transformed into awakening are also of that nature, having those characteristics.Thought is not known by thought, thought is not seen by thought, [past] thought does not [perish in the future, it does not] connect itself to [future] thought.

byaṅ chub kyi sems mi brjed pa de ji rtag pa’i phyir de lta bu źig nas mñam pa la de’i phyir de sems la ’di ltar rtog go źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs te, de źes bya ba ni sems dran pa ñe bar gźag pa la gnas pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de’am, byaṅ chub kyi sems ma brjed pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de’o; sems la źes bya ba ni byaṅ chub kyi sems la’o; ’di ltar rtog ces bya ba ni byaṅ chub kyi sems de la ’og nas ’byuṅ ba ltar rtog pa’o.ji ltar rtog ce na? de’i phyir sems ni skyes nas źig ciṅ źu ste źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs te, sems ni sems ñid dam byaṅ chub kyi sems so; skyes nas źes bya ba ni skyes ma thag tu’o, źig ciṅ źu ste źes bya ba ni don gcig ste, de ma thag tu źig pa’o, mi gnas pa ni cuṅ zad kyaṅ ’dug pa’i mthu med pa ste, de bas na źes bya bar sbyar ro; naṅ na yaṅ mi ’dug phyi rol tu yaṅ ma ’phos gñi ga med par yaṅ mi dmigs źes bya ba ni sems de skad ciṅ ma’i raṅ bźin skyes ma thag tu ’jig ciṅ deṅs pa’i ṅo bo ñid de, źig nas kyaṅ med par ’gyur ba’i, naṅ gi dṅos po rnams la yaṅ mi gnas, phyi rol gyi yul rnams la yaṅ ma ’phos, de gñis ka ma yin pa gźan phyi naṅ gñis kyi bar na yaṅ mi dmigs mi gnas śes bya ba’i don to.gaṅ bdag gi byaṅ chub kyi sems daṅ po skyes pa de ni źes bya ba la sogs pa la goṅ du bśad par yaṅ ma zad kyi ’di ltar yaṅ khoṅ du chud do źes bya bar sbyar ro, skyes pa ’das pa’i dus su skyes zin to, zad ciṅ ’gags pa la sogs pa ni don gcig ste med par gyur par ston pa’o, zad pa ni rgyu med pa’o, ’gags pa ni raṅ bźin gyis med pa’o, yal źiṅ med par gyur pas kyaṅ gaṅ du yaṅ gnas par ma gyur te, de bas na med par gyur pa źes bya’o, yul daṅ phyogs na mi gnas te de ni gdags par mi nus so źes bya ba la thog mar sems bskyed de, de skad cig ma re re la yaṅ zad ciṅ ’gags te, gźan daṅ gźan du gyur la, yal źiṅ med par gyur pas dṅos po med pa’i phyir yul daṅ phyogs na mi gnas miṅ daṅ mtshan mar gdags su med do źes bya ba’i don to.sems gaṅ dag gis byaṅ chub kyi phyir dge ba’i rtsa ba rnams yaṅ dag par bsgrubs pa, de dag kyaṅ zad ciṅ ’gags te yal źiṅ med par gyur pas źes bya ba la sems gaṅ dag gis ni dge ba’i rtsa ba’i rgyur ’gyur ba’i sems bskyed pa gaṅ gis so, byaṅ chub kyi phyir ni ’bras bu de thob par bya ba’i phyir ro, dge ba’i rtsa ba bsgrubs pa ni bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyi tshogs bsgrubs pa’o; byaṅ chub kyi sems ñid goṅ du smos pa’i tshul gyis don dam par ṅo bo ñid med pas, de las byuṅ ba’i dge ba’i rtsa ba rnams kyaṅ yal źiṅ med par gyur pas sems bźin du ṅo bo ñid med pa’i phyir yul daṅ phyogs na mi gnas śiṅ miṅ daṅ mtshan mar gdags par mi nus so źes bya ba’i don to.sems gaṅ dag gis dge ba’i rtsa ba de dag byaṅ chub tu bsṅos pa, de dag kyaṅ de’i raṅ bźin de’i mtshan ñid de źes bya ba la sems gaṅ gis ni dge ba’i rtsa ba’i rgyur gyur pa’i byaṅ chub kyi sems bskyed pas so, byaṅ chub tu bsṅos pas kyaṅ źes bya ba ni byaṅ chub tu bsṅos pa’i ’og tu ’phel bar gyur pa’i dge ba’i rtsa ba de dag go, de ni ’di skad du byaṅ chub kyi sems kyis bskyed pa’i dge ba’i rtsa ba bla na med pa’i byaṅ chub tu bṅos pa de dag kyaṅ sems daṅ ’dra bar zad ciṅ ’gags te yal źiṅ med par ’gyur ba’i mtshan ñid las yul daṅ phyogs na mi gnas śiṅ gdags par nus so źes bya bar ston to.sems kyis sems mi śes so źes bya ba ni sems de raṅ bźin med pas śes par byed pa daṅ śes par bya ba med pa’i phyir ro. de bźin du ’og ma la yaṅ sbyar ro; sems kyis sems kyi mtshams mi sbyor ba ni ’das pa’i sems de ma ’oṅs par mi ’pho źiṅ, ma ’oṅs pa’i sems daṅ mtshams mi sbyor ba’o.


de la sems gaṅ źe na?gaṅ gis bdag bla na med pa yaṅ dag par rdzogs pa’i byaṅ chub mṅon par rdzogs par ’tshaṅ rgya bar bya’o sñam pa ste.byaṅ chub kyi sems gaṅ yin pa, de dge ba’i rtsa ba’i sems daṅ lhan cig mi gnas, dge ba’i rtsa ba’i sems de yaṅ yoṅs su bsṅo ba’i sems daṅ lhan cig mi gnas; yoṅs su bsṅo ba’i sems gaṅ yin pa, de yaṅ byaṅ chub kyi sems daṅ lhan cig mi gnas so,sñam du rtog go.

tatra katamac cittam?yenāham anuttarāṃ samyaksaṃbodhim abhisaṃbhotsya iti.yad bodhicittaṃ tat kuśalamūlacittena sārdhaṃ na saṃvasati, tat punaḥ kuśalamūlacittaṃ pariṇāmacittena sārdhaṃ na saṃvasati.iti pratyavekṣate.

What then is thought?That by which one thinks: ‘I will wake up to the incomparable perfect awakening’.But the thought of awakening does not stay with the roots of good [since there is really (svabhāvatas) no attainment at all]; and again, [thus] the thought of the roots of the good does not stay with the thought of transformation.Thus he reflects.

de la gaṅ gis bdag bla na med pa yaṅ dag par rdzogs pa’i byaṅ chub mṅon par rdzogs par ’tshaṅ rgya bar bya źes bya ba la sogs pa la mṅon par ’tshaṅ rgya bar ’gyur ba’i byaṅ chub kyi sems daṅ dge ba’i rtsa ba’i sems la sogs pa goṅ du smos pa lta bu’i tshul gyis brtags na gaṅ yaṅ raṅ bźin du grub pa ni med pas byaṅ chub kyi sems gaṅ yin pa de dge ba’i rtsa ba’i sems daṅ lhan cig mi gnas źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so. (269a)


gal te byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de ltar rtog pa ni mi skrag mi dṅaṅ źiṅ dṅaṅ bar mi ’gyur na, sems la sems kyi rjes su lta źiṅ gnas so.

yadi bodhisattva evaṃ pratyavekṣamāno nottrasyen na saṃtrasyen na saṃtrāsam āpadyeta, citte cittānudarśī viharet.

If the bodhisattva reflecting thus does not get afraid, scared or terrified, he is one to continually consider thought.


yaṅ de ’di sñam du sems te:rgyu daṅ ’bras bu chud mi za ba’i phyir rten ciṅ ’brel bar ’byuṅ ba ni śin tu zab ste;sems kyi chos ñid de lta bu yin du zin kyaṅ, chos thams cad ni rgyu daṅ rkyen la rag las śiṅ g-yo ba med pa, byed pa med pa, bdag po med pa, yoṅs su ’dzin pa med pa ste;de dag ji ltar ’dod pa de bźin du rnam par sgrub bo. bdag gis kyaṅ rnam par sgrub pa la brtson par bya ste, sems kyi chos ñid ’di yaṅ mi spoṅ ṅo, sñam mo.

punas tasyaivaṃ bhavati:atigambhīraḥ pratītyasamutpādo hetuphalāvipraṇāśatayākiṃ cāpi cittadharmataivaṃ bhavet sarvadharmā hetupratyayādhīnā nirīhā niśceṣṭā asvāmikā aparigrahāḥ.tān yathecchaṃ viṭhapayeyam, viṭhapanāyāṃ mayā yogaḥ karaṇīyaḥ, iyaṃ ca cittadharmatā na vihātavyā.

Again he thinks:“[Thought is essentially empty (svabhāvaśūnya), but] dependent origination is very deep since there is no destruction of [the connection between] the cause [that which is performed] and the fruit [attained, i. e., according to conventional truth (saṃvṛtyā) ].Even though the true state of thought is such [that it is in the highest meaning (paramārthatas) essentially empty (svabhāvaśūnya) ], all moments of existence are dependent on causes and conditions, but without a mover, without an activator, without an owner, beyond appropriation.I will create them [the causes (hetu), like giving up all belongings, and the fruit (phala), purifying my buddha-field] according to my wish, and I will exert myself in creating [according to conventional truth (saṃvṛtyā) ], but I will not give up the true state of thought [which is unborn according to the highest truth (paramārthatas) ].

sems la rtog pa’i rnam pa gźan pa bstan pa’i phyir yaṅ de ’di sñam du sems te rgyu daṅ ’bras bu chud mi za ba źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so; de ni byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de’o; de ltar sems kyi raṅ bźin stoṅ pa yaṅ kun rdzob tu rgyu gaṅ spyad pa’i ’bras bu de bźin du thob par ’gyur te chud mi za bar rten ciṅ ’brel bar ’byuṅ ba gcig kho nar ṅes par mi mṅon pa de ni blta dka’ bas śin tu zab bo źes bya ba’i don to; sems kyi chos ñid de lta bu yin du zin kyaṅ źes bya ba la sogs pa ni don dam par sems de ṅo bo ñid kyis stoṅ du zin kyaṅ kun rdzob tu rgyu daṅ rkyen rnams tshogs na med par mi ’gyur la, rgyu rkyen tshogs pa las ’byuṅ ba de dag kyaṅ mu stegs can gyi byed pa po la sogs pa med pas de dag gis kyaṅ bskyod do źes bya ba’i don to; de dag ji ltar ’dod pa bźin rnam par bsgrub pa źes bya ba ni ji ltar byed pa po la sogs pa med par yaṅ rgyu rkyen tshogs pa las kun rdzob tu skye źiṅ ’byuṅ bas na rgyu gaṅ daṅ gaṅ gis ’bras bu gaṅ daṅ gaṅ sgrub pa de dag ji ltar ’dod pa bźin du grub źes bya ba’i don to. bdag gis kyaṅ rnam par bsgrub pa la brtson par bya źes bya ba ni ji ltar ’dod pa bźin ’bras bu ’grub pas bdag gis kyaṅ bdog pa thams cad yoṅs su gtaṅ ba la sogs pa’i ’bras bu saṅs rgyas kyi źiṅ yoṅs su dag par bya ba’i phyir bsṅo ba la sogs pa la brtson par bya’o źes bya ba’i don to; sems kyi chos ñid ’di yaṅ mi spoṅ ṅo źes bya ba ni de dag kun rdzob tu sgrub pa la brtson par byed kyaṅ, don dam par sems kyi chos ñid ma skyes pa yaṅ mi spoṅ ba’am, de yaṅ ñams par mi byed pa’o; sems ni sgyu ma lta bu ste źes bya ba la sogs pas pha rol tu phyin pa drug don dam par mi dmigs pa daṅ, kun rdzob tu rgyu daṅ ’bras bu ṅes par yod par bstan pa’o.


de la sems kyi chos ñid ni gaṅ, rnam par sgrub pa ni gaṅ źe na?

tatra katamā cittadharmatā, katamā viṭhapanā?

(p. 131) What then is the true state of thought, what is creating?


sems ni sgyu ma lta bu ste, ’di la gaṅ sbyin par byed pa gaṅ yaṅ med de, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.gaṅ yaṅ bdog pa thams cad yoṅs su btaṅ ste, saṅs rgyas kyi źiṅ yoṅs su dag par bya ba’i phyir yoṅs su sṅo ba, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

māyopamaṃ cittam iha nāsti dātā kaścaneyam ucyate cittadharmatā,yat punaḥ sarvasvaṃ parityajya sarvabuddhakṣetrapariśuddhaye pariṇāmayatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought is like an illusion – here there is no giver at all – this is called the true state of thought,but when one gives up all belongings and transforms it so as to purify one’s buddha-field, it is called creating.

sgyu ma lta bur źes bya ba ni sgyu ma bźin du raṅ bźin med pa ste, de bas na gaṅ zag ’di la bdag gis dṅos po gtoṅ bar snaṅ ba ni raṅ bźin med pa’o; bdog pa thams cad yoṅs su btaṅ ste źes bya ba ni phyi daṅ naṅ gi dṅos po thams cad yoṅs su gtoṅ ba’o, saṅs rgyas kyi źiṅ dag pa ni bai dū rya’i kha dog la sogs pa’o.


sems ni rmi lam lta bur rab tu źi ba’i mtshan ñid de, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.gaṅ yaṅ tshul khrims daṅ, bslab pa daṅ, sbyaṅs pa’i yon tan daṅ, byaṅ chub kyi tshogs sogs pa thams cad mkhyen pa ñid kyi phyir yoṅs su sṅo ba, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

svapnopamaṃ cittam praśāntalakṣaṇam iyam ucyate cittadharmatā,yat punaḥ śīlaśikṣādhūtaguṇabodhisaṃbhāropacayaṃ sarvajñatāyai pariṇāmayatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought is like a dream, characterized by absolute peace, this is called the true state of thought,but when one transforms morality, training and qualities of purification into omniscience – gathering the accumulation for awakening – it is called creating.

sems rmi lam lta bu źes bya ba la, ji ltar rmi lam na dṅos po rnam pa sna tshogs snaṅ yaṅ raṅ bźin stoṅ pa de bźin du sems la gzuṅ ba daṅ ’dzin pa la sogs pa rnam pa du mar snaṅ yaṅ raṅ bźin gyis stoṅ pa’o; yaṅ dag par blaṅs pa daṅ yaṅ dag par slob pa ni tshul khrims so, yaṅ dag par blaṅ bar bya ba daṅ bslab par bya ba ni bslab pa’o, thams cad mkhyen pa ñid kyi phyir źes bya ba ni thams cad mkhyen pa thob par bya ba’i phyir ro.


sems ni mig yor lta bu ste, gzod ma nas yoṅs su ma grub pa, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.gaṅ yaṅ bzod pa daṅ des pa thams cad mi skye ba’i chos la bzod pa yoṅs su rdzogs par bya ba’i phyir yoṅs su sṅo ba, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

pratibhāsopamaṃ cittam ādyapariniṣpannam iyam ucyate cittadharmatā,yat punaḥ sarvakṣāntisauratyam anutpattikadharmakṣāntiparipūraye pariṇāmayatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought is like a reflection, from the beginning not developed, this is called the true state of thought,but when one transforms all tolerance and mildness into attaining the tolerance that all moments of existence are unborn, it is called creating.

gzod ma nas yoṅs su ma grub ces bya ba ni ji ltar mig yor phyis brtags te, raṅ bźin med pa ltar daṅ po snaṅ ba’i dus na yaṅ raṅ bźin med pa yin no, de daṅ ’dra bar sems ’di yaṅ thog ma nas raṅ bźin med ciṅ ma skyes pa’o, bzod pa ni gźan gyis gnod pa byas pa ñams su len pa’o, des pa ni slar gnod pa mi byed pa ste, de dag bsgrubs pa źes bya bar sbyar ro; mi skye ba’i chos la bzod pa rdzogs par bya ba’i phyir źes bya ba ni skye ba daṅ ’gag pa med pa’i chos la skrag pa med pa thob par bya ba’i phyir bsṅo ba’o.


sems ni smig rgyu lta bu ste, śin tu dben pa’i phyir dben pa, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.gaṅ yaṅ dge ba’i rtsa ba thams cad la brtson ’grus brtsams pa rnams saṅs rgyas kyi chos thams cad yoṅs su rdzogs par bya ba’i phyir yoṅs su sṅo ba, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

marīcyupamaṃ cittam viviktam atyantavivekatayeyam ucyate cittadharmatā,yat punaḥ sarvakuśalamūlavīryārambhān sarvabuddhadharmaparipūraye pariṇāmayatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought is like a mirage [empty of water], dissociated [from and empty of subject and object (grāhyagrāhaka) ] because of its absolute dissociation, this is called the true state of thought,but when one transforms the undertakings of vigour for [attaining] all roots of the good into fulfilling all teachings of the Buddha, it is called creating.

śin tu dben pa źes bya ba ni smig rgyu chus stoṅ ste, dben pa bźin du sems la yaṅ gzuṅ ba daṅ ’dzin pas stoṅ ste dben pa’o; dge ba’i rtsa ba thams cad la źes bya ba ni dge ba’i rtsa ba thams cad sgrub pa la źe’am, sgrub pa’i phyir źes bya ba’i don to, saṅs rgyas kyi chos ni sa daṅ pha rol tu phyin pa la sogs pa’o.


sems ni gzugs can ma yin pa, bstan du med pa, thogs pa med pa, rnam par rig pa med pa ste, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.gaṅ yaṅ bsam gtan daṅ, rnam par thar pa daṅ, tiṅ ṅe ’dzin daṅ, sñoms par ’jug pa thams cad saṅs rgyas kyi tiṅ ṅe ’dzin thob par bya ba’i phyir yoṅs su sṅo ba, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

arūpi cittam anidarśanam apratihatam avijñāptikam iyam ucyate cittadharmatā,yat punaḥ sarvadhyānavimokṣasamādhisamāpattayo buddhasamādhiprāptaye pariṇāmayatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought is without form, it cannot be demonstrated, is unhindered [and all-pervasive (sarvatragata) like empty space (ākāśa) ] and beyond communication, this is called the true state of thought,but when one transforms all meditations, liberations, concentrations and the states of concentration into attaining the concentration of a Buddha [that is, the concentration beyond thought-constructions (nirvikalpasamādhi) ], it is called creating.

gzugs can ma yin pa źes bya ba ni thogs pa daṅ bcas pa’i gzugs kyi ṅo bo ma yin pa’i phyir ro, bstan du med pa źes bya ba ni sṅon po la sogs pa bźin du gźan la bstan par mi nus pa’o, thogs pa med pa ni nam mkha’ bźin du kun tu soṅ ba’i phyir ro, rnam par rig pa med pa ni rig par bya ba daṅ rig par byed pa daṅ bral ba’i phyir ro; saṅs rgyas kyi tiṅ ṅe ’dzin ni rnam par mi rtog pa’i tiṅ ṅe ’dzin no.


sems ni gzuṅ ba med pa, mtshan ma med pa, bstan du med pa, gnas med pa ste, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.gaṅ yaṅ dris pa’i tshig rab tu dbye ba bstan pa śes pa thams cad saṅs rgyas kyi ye śes yoṅs su rdzogs par bya ba’i phyir yoṅs su sṅo ba, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

agrāhyaṃ cittam animittam anidarśanam anavasthitam iyam ucyate cittadharmatā,yat punaḥ sarvapraśnapadaprabhedasaṃdarśanajñānaṃ buddhajñānaparipūraye pariṇāmayatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought cannot be grasped; signless, it cannot be demonstrated, it has no basis, this is called the true state of thought,but when one transforms the knowledge of explaining by analysis all words of questions into fulfilling the knowledge of a Buddha, it is called creating.

gzuṅ ba med pa ni sems daṅ sems las byuṅ ba’i gzuṅ bar snaṅ ba dṅos por ma grub pa’i phyir ro, mtshan ma med pa ni gzugs la sogs pa’i ṅo bo ma yin pa’i phyir ro, bstan du med pa ni sṅa ma bźin no, gnas med pa ni gzugs daṅ dbaṅ po la mi gnas pa’i phyir ro; dris pa’i tshig ni phuṅ po daṅ khams la sogs pa gźan gyis dris pa’o, dbye ba bstan pa śes pa źes bya ba ni ji ltar thos pa’i chos rnams so sor phye ste bśad pa’i gnas śes rab bo, saṅs rgyas kyi ye śes ni me loṅ lta bu la sogs pa’o.


sems ni dmigs pa med par mi skye ba ste, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.saṅs rgyas kyi chos thams cad thob par bya ba’i phyir dge ba’i rtsa ba thams cad la dmigs pa’i sems ñe bar sgrub pa, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

anārambaṇaṃ cittam notpadyata iyam ucyate cittadharmatā;yat punaḥ sarvabuddhadharmaprāpaṇatayā sarvakuśalamūlārambaṇaṃ cittam upasaṃharatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought does not originate without an object [that is, without the causes and conditions of objects (ālambanahetupratyaya) ], this is called the true state of thought;when one produces thoughts that have all roots of good as the object for the sake of attaining all the qualities of a Buddha, it is called creating.

sems ni dmigs pa med par mi skye źes bya ba la sogs pas kun rdzob kyi sems kyis dmigs pa’i rgyu daṅ rkyen la sogs pa yod par bstan pa las sgrub pa yaṅ yod par bstan pa ste, dmigs par bya ba’i yul med par sems mi skye’o, yul la dmigs nas skye ba ni sems kyi chos ñid do; gaṅ dge ba’i rtsa ba thams cad la dmigs pa źes bya ba ni sems yul la dmigs nas skye ba yin pas, dmigs nas yaṅ dge ba’i rtsa ba skye bar ’gyur ba’i dmigs pa thams cad la sems dmigs pa’o.


sems ni rgyu med par mi skye ba ste, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.gaṅ yaṅ byaṅ chub kyi phyogs kyi chos thams cad kyi rgyur sems bskyed pa, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

ahetukaṃ cittam notpadyata iyam ucyate cittadharmatā,yat punaḥ sarvabodhipakṣikadharmahetoś cittam utpādayatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought does not originate without a cause, this is called the true state of thought,but when one produces thoughts for the sake of [that is, being the cause of] all the moments of existence that are wings of awakening, it is called creating.

rgyu med par mi skye ba ni kun gźi rnam par śes pa’i bag chags med par sems mi skye ba’o – "Thought does not originate without the habits (vāsanā) of the store-consciousness (ālayavijñāna)". byaṅ chub kyi phyogs kyi chos źes bya ba ni sems skye ba’i rgyu bskyed ciṅ ’jog na yaṅ byaṅ chub kyi phyogs kyi chos skye ba’i rgyu bskyed pa’o.


yul drug las rnam par grol bar sems mi skye ba, ’di ni sems kyi chos ñid ces bya’o.gaṅ yaṅ saṅs rgyas kyi chos thams cad kyi yul gyi rgyur sems mṅon par sgrub pa, ’di ni rnam par sgrub pa źes bya’o.

ṣaḍviṣayavimuktaṃ cittam notpadyata iyam ucyate cittadharmatā,yat punaḥ sarvabuddhadharmaviṣayahetoś cittam abhinirharatīyam ucyate viṭhapanā.

Thought does not originate without the six object-fields of sense-perception, this is called the true state of thought,but when one produces thoughts [namely the moments of existence of insight and expedient means (prajñopāyadharma) ] for the sake of [that is, being the cause of, in that they produce] the field of all the teachings [and qualities] of a Buddha [this field, however, being emptiness (śūnyatā) ], it is called creating.

yul drug la źes bya ba ni yul drug la ma brten par sems mi skye ba’o; saṅs rgyas kyi chos thams cad ni sa daṅ pha rol tu phyin pa daṅ stobs daṅ mi ’jigs pa la sogs pa’o, de dag gi yul ni stoṅ pa ñid do, rgyu ni yul te, mṅon du byed pa’i chos thabs daṅ śes rab bo; sgrub pa ni thabs daṅ śes rab sgrub pa’o.


sems la sems kyi rjes su lta źiṅ gnas pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ mṅon par śes pa thob par bya ba’i phyir sems ñe bar ’dogs so.de mṅon par śes pa thob pa’i sems kyis sems can thams cad kyi sems kyi spyod pa rab tu śes te.śes nas kyaṅ de lta bu’i raṅ bźin gyi chos ston to.sems la sems kyi rjes su lta źiṅ gnas pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ sñiṅ rje chen po thob par bya ba’i phyir sems ñe bar ’dogs te, de sñiṅ rje chen po thob pa’i sems kyis sems can thams cad yoṅs su smin par byed pas mi skyo’o.sems la sems kyi rjes su lta źiṅ gnas pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ sems zad par bya ba’i phyir sems mi skyed, sems ’gog par bya ba’i phyir yaṅ ma yin te;’khor ba’i rgyun gyi dge ba’i rtsa ba kun tu sbyor ba rnams la sems mṅon par sgrub bo.sems kyi dran pa śes pa des ma byuṅ ba daṅ, mi skye ba daṅ, mi ’gog par ṅes par ’gyur ba la ’jug pa’i chos su gnas kyaṅ, ñan thos daṅ raṅ saṅs rgyas kyi sar mi ltuṅ ste;nam yaṅ saṅs rgyas kyi chos thams cad yoṅs su rdzogs par ma gyur gyi bar du, sems kyi rgyun de ñe bar ’dogs te;sems kyi skad cig gcig daṅ ldan pa’i śes rab kyis bla na med pa yaṅ dag par rdzogs pa’i byaṅ chub kyaṅ mṅon par rdzogs par ’tshaṅ rgya ste,

citte cittānudarśī viharan bodhisattvo ’bhijñāprāptaye cittam upacarati;so ’bhijñāprāptena cittena sarvasattvānāṃ cittacaritāni prajānāti.jñatvā caivaṃsvabhāvena dharmaṃ deśayati.citte cittānudarśī viharan bodhisattvo mahākaruṇāprāptaye cittam upacarati, so mahākaruṇāprāptena cittena sarvasattvaparipācanena na parikhidyate.citte cittānudarśī viharan bodhisattvo na cittakṣayāya cittam utpādayati nāpi cittanirodhāya,samsārasrotasaḥ kuśalamūlasaṃyojaneṣu cittam abhinirharati.tena cittasmṛtijñānenābhūtānutpādānirodhaniyāmapraveśadharme sthito ’pi na śrāvakapratyekabuddhabhūmau patati.yāvat kadācit sarvabuddhadharmaparipūraṇāc cittasaṃtānam upacarati;ekakṣaṇasamāyuktayā prajñayānuttarāṃ samyaksaṃbodhim abhisaṃbudhyate.

The bodhisattva continually considering thought treats [and thus makes one-pointed (ekāgra) ] thought so as to attain supernormal knowledge;and having attained supernormal knowledge by means of thought he knows the mental behaviour of all beings.Having known this he teaches them religion in accordance with their nature.The bodhisattva continually considering thought treats thought so as to attain great compassion; and having attained great compassion by means of thought he never gets tired of maturing all beings.The bodhisattva continually considering thought does not produce the thought that thought should perish, or that thought should cease [like the disciples and isolated buddhas intent solely on peace (śānti), on extinction (nirvāṇa); on the contrary, by expedient means (upāya) he does not cut off the roots of good (kuśalamūla) in the stream of existence (saṃsārasrotas) ],(p. 132) but he produces the thought to join himself to the roots of good in the stream of existence.Though he by that knowledge of recollection of thought is situated in the moment of existence which is the entrance into the certainty that all is unborn, does not arise and does not cease, he does not fall into the stage of disciples and isolated buddhas.And until the fulfilment of all qualities of a Buddha [that is, the incomparable complete awakening (anuttarasamyaksaṃbodhi) for oneself and all other beings], he relates at any time to the continuity of thoughts [never giving up the continuity of thoughts which is the union of insight and expedient means (prajñôpâyayuganaddha) ];and he wakes up to incomparable, perfect awakening by insight associated with that moment.

mṅon par śes pa ni drug rnams so, sems ñe bar ’dogs pa ni rtse gcig tu ’jog pa’o.sems kyi spyod pa rab tu śes pa’o sems can rnams kyi sems ji lta bu yin pa daṅ, ’dod chags la sogs pa’i ñon moṅs pa gaṅ la spyod pa śes pa’o.de lta bu’i raṅ bźin gyis chos ston pa ni ñon moṅs pa gaṅ la spyod pa daṅ de daṅ mthun pa’i chos mi gtsaṅ ba la sogs pa ston pa’o.mi skyo ba ni sñiṅ rje daṅ ldan pas so.sems zad par bya ba’i phyir sems mi skyed ces bya ba la sogs pa ni ñan thos daṅ raṅ saṅs rgyas ltar źi ba’i phyogs gcig pa’i mya ṅan las ’das pa la gnas pa ni ma yin gyi, thabs kyis yoṅs su zin pas ’khor ba’i rgyun gyi dge ba’i rtsa ba mi gcod do źes bya ba’i don to. zad pa ni ñon moṅs pa zad pa’i phyir ro; ’gog pa ni phuṅ po spaṅ ba’i phyir ro.sems kyi dran pa śes pa des źes bya ba la sogs pas goṅ du smos pa lta bu’i sems dran pa ñe bar ’jog pa de khoṅ du chud de, des ma byuṅ ba daṅ mi skye ba la sogs pa’i mtshan ñid la gnas kyaṅ chos ñid de’i dbaṅ du ma gyur pas ñan thos daṅ raṅ saṅs rgyas dag ltar ’khor ba yoṅs su spoṅ bar yaṅ mi ’gyur ro źes bya ba’i don to.nam saṅs rgyas kyi chos źes bya ba la sogs pa ni bdag daṅ sems can thams cad bla na med pa’i byaṅ chub tu ma grub kyi bar du thabs daṅ śes rab zuṅ du ’brel ba’i sems kyi rgyun mi gtoṅ ba’o.sems kyi skad cig gcig daṅ ldan pa’i śes rab ces bya ba ni mthar mṅon par byaṅ chub pa la ’jug pa’i sems kyi skad cig ma gcig daṅ ldan pa, bdag med pa gñis khoṅ du chud pa’i śes rab kyis de bźin ñid mṅon sum du byed pa’o. This is called the bodhisattvas’ presence of recollection which consists in the consideration of thought.
