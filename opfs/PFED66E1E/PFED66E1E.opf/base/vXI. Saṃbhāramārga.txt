XI. Saṃbhāramārga

XI. The Path of Accumulation


btsun pa śa ra dva ti’i bu, gźan yaṅ byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyi tshogs kyaṅ mi zad pa yin te.

punar aparam, bhadanta śāradvatīputra, bodhisattvānāṃ puṇyajñānasaṃbhārāv apy akṣayau.

Further, reverend Śāradvatīputra, the bodhisattvas’ accumulations of merit and knowledge are also imperishable.

yoṅs su byaṅ ba’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ bstan pa’i phyir btsun pa śa ra dva ti’i bu gźan yaṅ byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyi tshogs kyaṅ mi zad pa źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs te, de dag mi zad pa yaṅ bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyis spyad pa’i yul sbyin pa la sogs pa mi zad pa’i phyir ro – "To teach what the purified (paryavadāta) bodhisattva is, it is said ... They are imperishable (akṣaya) since the sphere (gocara), i. e. generosity, etc. (dānādi), where merit (puṇya) and knowledge (jñāna) are practised is imperishable (akṣaya)."
yoṅs su byaṅ ba’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ yaṅ rnam pa gñis te, rtag par phoṅs pa med pa daṅ, the tshom thams cad gcod pa’o. tshogs gñis kyis bsdus pa’i don tshogs kyi lam bstan par yaṅ sbyar ro. de la rtag par phoṅs pa med pa gaṅ źe na? gaṅ gis bsod nams kyi tshogs bsags pa’o; the tshom thams cad gcod pa gaṅ źe na? gaṅ gis ye śes kyi tshogs bsags pa’o.
