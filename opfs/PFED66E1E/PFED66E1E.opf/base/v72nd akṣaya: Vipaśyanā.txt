72nd akṣaya: Vipaśyanā

72nd Imperishable: Expanded Vision.


de la byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi lhag mthoṅ mi zad pa gaṅ źe na?

tatra katamā bodhisattvānāṃ vipaśyanākṣayatā?

What then is the imperishability of expanded vision?

spros pa’i rnal ’byor la źugs pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ ni gaṅ lam de dag ñid na lhag mthoṅ la rab tu sbyor ba ste, de’i dbaṅ du byas nas de la byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi lhag mthoṅ mi zad pa gaṅ źe na źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so – "As for the bodhisattva progressing in the practice of effort (utsāhayogapratipanna), he is the one practising expanded vision on those same ways [of practice, vision and cultivation] (yas teṣv eva mārgeṣu vipaśyanāprayuktaḥ); with reference to him (tam ādhipatiṃ kṛtvā) it is said: ...


gaṅ śes rab kyi cha daṅ ’thun pa sgom pa’i lam ste;chos rnams la g-yo ba med par śes pa daṅ;bdag med pa daṅ, sems can med pa daṅ, srog med pa daṅ, gso ba med pa daṅ, gaṅ zag med par śes pa daṅ;phuṅ po rnams la sgyu mar mthoṅ ba’i śes pa daṅ,khams rnams la sbrul gdug pa lta bur mthoṅ ba’i śes pa daṅ,skye mched rnams la groṅ stoṅ pa lta bur mthoṅ ba’i śes pa daṅ;bden pa rnams la śin tu rtogs par mthoṅ ba’i śes pa daṅ;rten ciṅ ’brel bar ’byuṅ ba daṅ mi ’gal bar mthoṅ ba’i śes pa daṅ;lta ba thams cad daṅ bral bar mthoṅ ba’i śes pa daṅ;rgyu daṅ ’bras bu daṅ rnam par smin pa chud mi za bar mthoṅ ba’i śes pa daṅ;’bras bu ’thob pa mṅon sum du bya ba la ṅes par mthoṅ ba’i śes pa daṅ;yaṅ dag pa ñid la ’jug pa mthoṅ ba’i śes pa ni lhag mthoṅ źes bya ste.

yaḥ prajñābhāgīyo bhāvanāmārgaḥ;dharmeṣu nirīhajñānaṃ;nairātmyaniḥsattvanirjīvaniḥpoṣaniḥpudgalajñānaṃ;skandheṣu māyādarśanajñānaṃ;dhātuṣv āśīviṣadarśanajñānam;āyataneṣu śūnyagrāmadarśanajñānaṃ;satyeṣu supratividdhadarśanajñānaṃ;pratītyasamutpādāviruddhadarśanajñānaṃ;sarvadṛṣṭivigatadarśanajñānaṃ;hetuphalavipākāvipraṇāśadarśanajñānaṃ;phalaprāptisākṣātkriyāpratyavekṣādarśanajñānaṃ;samyaktvāvakrāntidarśanajñānaṃ vipaśyaneti.

It is the way of cultivation connected to insight [since expanded vision is the essence of insight (prajñāsvabhāva), it is all practice (abhyāsa) directed towards insight];knowledge of the immovability of moments of existence;[as there is no mover (kāraka), thus it is] knowledge of selflessness, no being, no life-principle, no life-supporting principle, no personality;knowledge seeing the parts of personality as an illusion;knowledge seeing the spheres of sense-perception as snake-poison;knowledge seeing the fields of sense-perception as an empty village;knowledge seeing the [four holy] truths as well understood;knowledge seeing things not in contradiction to dependent origination [that is, seeing all moments of existence as arising from causes and conditions (sarvadharmāṇāṃ hetupratyayôtpādadarśanam) ];knowledge seeing that which is beyond any viewpoint;knowledge seeing that the maturation of the fruit from its cause will never cease [as one performs action, one will accordingly experience its maturation];knowledge seeing through inspection the realization of the attainment of the fruit [of accumulating merit and knowledge (puṇyajñānasaṃbhāra), namely the extinction which is found nowhere (apratiṣṭhitanirvāṇa) ];knowledge seeing the attainment of truth [namely emptiness (śūnyatā) ] is expanded vision.

gaṅ śes rab kyi cha daṅ mthun par bsgom pa’i lam źes bya ba la lhag mthoṅ ni śes rab kyi raṅ bźin yin pas, śes rab kyi cha daṅ phyogs su gtogs pa’i chos la goms par byed pa thams cad lhag mthoṅ źes bya’o.chos rnams la g-yo ba med par śes pa ni chos thams cad la byed pa po med par śes pa ste, goṅ du bźad pa rnams ni ’dir ma bśad do.bden pa rnams la śin tu rtogs par mthoṅ ba’i śes pa źes bya ba ni sdug bsṅal daṅ kun ’byuṅ ba daṅ ’gog pa daṅ lam gyi bden pa rnams la bden pa so so’i mtshan ñid gaṅ yin pa phyin ci ma log par rtogs śiṅ mthoṅ ba ni śin tu rtogs par mthoṅ ba’i śes pa źes bya’o.rten ciṅ ’brel bar ’byuṅ ba źes bya ba la chos thams cad rgyu daṅ rkyen las skye bar mthoṅ ba ni rten ciṅ ’brel bar ’byuṅ ba daṅ mi ’gal bar mthoṅ ba’i śes pa’o.lta ba thams cad daṅ źes bya ba la chos thams cad rtag pa daṅ chad pa daṅ, bdag daṅ sems can med par mthoṅ ba ni lta ba thams cad daṅ bral bar mthoṅ ba’i śes pa źes bya’o.rgyu daṅ ’bras bu źes bya ba la rgyu gaṅ bskrun na ’bras bu de bźin du skye bar ’gyur ba daṅ, las gaṅ byas na rnam par smin pa de bźin du myoṅ bar ’gyur ba mthoṅ ba ni rgyu daṅ ’bras bu daṅ rnam par smin pa chud mi za bar mthoṅ ba’i śes pa’o.’bras bu ’thob pa źes bya ba la bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyi tshogs bsags pa’i ’bras bu mi gnas pa’i mya ṅan las ’das pa mṅon du byed ciṅ khoṅ du chud do źes mthoṅ ba ni ’bras bu ’thob pa mṅon sum du bya ba la rtog par mthoṅ ba’i śes pa’o.yaṅ dag pa ñid la ’jug pa ni ye śes te, ye śes kyis stoṅ pa ñid rtogs so źes mthoṅ ba ni yaṅ dag pa ñid la ’jug pa mthoṅ ba’i śes pa źes bya’o.


lhag mthoṅ źes bya ba ni chos rnams yaṅ dag par mthoṅ ba’o;chos rnams ji lta ba bźin du mthoṅ ba’o;chos rnams de kho na ñid du mthoṅ ba’o;chos rnams gźan ma yin pa ñid du mthoṅ ba’o;chos rnams stoṅ pa daṅ, mtshan ma med pa daṅ, smon pa med par mthoṅ ba’o;chos rnams mṅon par ’du mi byed pa daṅ, de bźin du ma skyes pa daṅ, ma byuṅ ba daṅ, dṅos po med pa daṅ, ji lta ba bźin daṅ, rnam par dag pa daṅ, dben par mthoṅ ba’o;chos rnams g-yo ba med pa daṅ, byed pa med pa daṅ, bdag po med pa daṅ, yoṅs su ’dzin pa med pa daṅ, dbyer med ciṅ ro gcig pa daṅ, nam mkha’i ṅo bo ñid daṅ, raṅ bźin gyis mya ṅan las ’das par mthoṅ ba’o.

vipaśyaneti dharmāṇāṃ samyagdarśanaṃ,dharmāṇāṃ yathābhūtadarśanaṃ,dharmāṇāṃ tattvadarśanaṃ,dharmāṇām ananyathātvadarśanaṃ,dharmāṇām śūnyānimitta-apraṇihitadarśanaṃ,dharmāṇām anabhisaṃskārayathājātānutpādābhāvayathābhūtaviśuddhivivekadarśanaṃ,dharmāṇām nirīhaniḥceṣṭāsvāmikāparigrahāsaṃbhedaikarasākāśasvabhāvaprakṛtiparinirvṛtidarśanaṃ.

[Further] expanded vision is truly seeing the moments of existence,seeing the moments of existence as they are,seeing the essence of moments of existence,seeing that the moments of existence are as they are and not otherwise,seeing that the moments of existence are empty, without distinguishing marks and nothing to be longed for,seeing that the moments of existence are unconditioned, unborn, unoriginated, inexistent, just as they are, pure [in being originally brilliant (prakṛtiprabhāsvarāḥ) ] and separated [from and thus empty of the forms of subject and object (grāhyagrāhâkāraiḥ śūnyāḥ) ],seeing that the moments of existence are without [absolute] movement, without a mover, without an owner, not possible to own, undifferentiated, of one taste, having the essence of space, originally extinguished.

lhag mthoṅ gi rnam graṅs gźan yaṅ bstan pa’i phyir lhag mthoṅ źes bya ba ni chos rnams yaṅ dag par mthoṅ ba’o źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs te, gaṅ zag daṅ chos la bdag med pa’i mtshan ñid du chos thams cad la phyin ci ma log par mthoṅ ba ni lhag mthoṅ ṅo.chos rnams la ji lta ba bźin du źes bya ba la kun rdzob tu chos thams cad sgyu ma daṅ smig rgyu tsam du ji ltar snaṅ ba de bźin du yod par mthoṅ ba’o; chos rnams de kho na ñid du mthoṅ źes bya ba ni phuṅ po la sogs pa’i chos rnams nam mkha’ bźin du ’gyur ba med pa’i raṅ bźin du mthoṅ ba’o; chos rnams gźan ma yin pa ñid du mthoṅ źes bya ba ni stoṅ pa ñid ’ba’ źig de kho na yin gyi, de las de kho na gźan med par mthoṅ ba’o.ji lta ba bźin daṅ rnam par dag pa daṅ dben par mthoṅ ba źes bya ba ni chos thams cad raṅ bźin gyis ’od gsal ba yin pa daṅ, gzuṅ ba daṅ ’dzin pa’i rnam pas stoṅ pa yin par mthoṅ ba’o.


lhag mthoṅ źes bya ba niyod pa’i rnam par yaṅ mi mthoṅ,med pa’i rnam par yaṅ mi mthoṅ;skye ba daṅ, ’jig pa daṅ, gnas pa’i rnam par yaṅ mi mthoṅ;dmigs pa daṅ mtshan ma’i rnam par yaṅ mi mthoṅ ba ste;de mthoṅ mod kyi, ci yaṅ mi mthoṅ ṅo.gaṅ gi tshe mthoṅ ba med pa, de’i tshe mthoṅ ste;lhag mthoṅ gis kyaṅ mthoṅ ba yaṅ ma yin, ma mthoṅ ba yaṅ ma yin par mthoṅ ṅo.gaṅ gi tshe de ltar mthoṅ ba, de’i tshe yaṅ dag pa ñid mthoṅ ṅo; yaṅ dag pa ñid mthoṅ bas lhag mthoṅ la mkhas pa ’thob par ’gyur ro.de la byaṅ chub sems dpa’ lhag mthoṅ gis de ltar mthoṅ yaṅ mṅon par ’du mi byed par yaṅ mi ltuṅ źiṅ dge ba’i rtsa ba mṅon par ’du byed pa las kyaṅ mi ldaṅ ste.

vipaśyaneti,nāpi bhāvākāraṃ paśyatināpy abhavākāraṃ paśyati;nāpy utpādasthitibhaṅgākāraṃ paśyati;nāpy ālambananimittākāraṃ paśyati.na sa paśyann api kiṃcit paśyati,yadā nāsti paśyanā tadā paśyati;vipaśyanayā ca na paśyan nāpy apaśyan paśyati.yadaivaṃ paśyati tadā samyaktvaṃ paśyati, samyaktvapaśyanayā vipaśyanākauśalyaṃ prāpnoti.tatra bodhisattvo vipaśyanayaivaṃ paśyann apy anabhisaṃskāre na patati, kuśalamūlābhisaṃskārāc ca nottiṣṭhati.

[Further] as for expanded vision,he does not see any form [existent own-being (svabhāva) or really existent state (bhāva) ] of existence,does not see any form [own-being (svabhāva) or really existent state (bhāva) ] of non-existence;does not see any form of originating, remaining and disappearing;does not see any form [or own-being (svabhāva) ] of objects [like form, sound, etc. (rūpaśabdâdi) ] nor of distinguishing marks [like white, red, etc. (śuklalohitādi) ].Though seeing he sees nothing,he sees when there is no seeing;(p. 148) by expanded vision he sees neither seeing nor non-seeing.When there is such seeing he sees truth, and by seeing the truth he attains skill in expanded vision [that is, skill with the essential characteristic (lakṣaṇa) of moments of existence].But the bodhisattva seeing by expanded vision does not fall into the unconditioned and does not leave off bringing the roots of good about.

rnam graṅs gźan yaṅ bstan pa’i phyir lhag mthoṅ źes bya ba ni źes bya ba la sogs pa gsuṅs so.yod pa’i rnam pa yaṅ mi mthoṅ źes bya ba ni yod pa’i raṅ bźin daṅ ṅo bor yaṅ mi mthoṅ ba’o.med pa’i rnam yaṅ mi mthoṅ źes bya ba ni med pa’i raṅ bźin daṅ ṅo bor yaṅ mi mthoṅ ba’o.dmigs pa daṅ mtshan ma źes bya ba la gzugs daṅ sgra la sogs pa’i dmigs pa daṅ, dkar po daṅ dmar po la sogs pa’i mtshan ma’i raṅ bźin yin par yaṅ mi mthoṅ ba’o.de mthoṅ mod kyi ci yaṅ mi mthoṅ ṅo źes bya ba ni lhag mthoṅ daṅ ldan pa’i byaṅ chub sems dpa’ de goṅ du miṅ gis smos pa’i chos rnams mi dmigs pa’i tshul du mthoṅ mod kyi, dmigs pa daṅ yod pa’i rnam pa rdul tsam yaṅ mi mthoṅ ṅo źes bya ba’i don to. gaṅ gi tshe mthoṅ ba med pa de’i tshe mthoṅ ste źes bya ba ni de ltar ci yaṅ mi dmigs te, don dam par ci yaṅ ma mthoṅ ba de ni chos ñid mthoṅ źiṅ rtogs so źes bya ba’i don to. lhag mthoṅ gis kyaṅ mthoṅ ba ma yin ma mthoṅ ba yaṅ ma yin par mthoṅ źes bya ba ni nam stoṅ par mthoṅ ba’i dus na dṅos po rdul tsam yaṅ ma mthoṅ bas mthoṅ ba yaṅ ma yin, de kho na mthoṅ bas na ma mthoṅ ba yaṅ ma yin pa’am, don dam par dṅos po gaṅ yaṅ ma mthoṅ bas na mthoṅ ba yaṅ ma yin la, kun rdzob tu btags pa tsam du mthoṅ bas na ma mthoṅ ba yaṅ ma yin pa’o.yaṅ dag pa ñid ces bya ba ni chos rnams kyi mtshan ñid phyin ci ma log pa ñid ces bya ba’i don to. lhag mthoṅ la mkhas pa thob par ’gyur ro źes bya ba ni chos rnams kyi mtshan ñid la mkhas pa’i lhag mthoṅ thob par ’gyur ro.de ltar mthoṅ yaṅ mṅon par ’du mi byed par mi ltuṅ źes bya ba ni don dam par goṅ du bśad pa lta bur chos rnams mi dmigs par mthoṅ yaṅ stoṅ pa ñid kyi mthar yaṅ mi ltuṅ źes bya ba’i don to; dge ba’i rtsa ba mṅon par ’du byed pa las kyaṅ mi ldaṅ źes bya ba ni kun rdzob tu las daṅ ’bras bu yod par lta bas bsod nams daṅ ye śes kyi tshogs mṅon par ’du byed pa yaṅ mi gtoṅ ṅo źes bya ba’i don to.


’di ni byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi lhag mthoṅ mi zad pa źes bya’o.

ayam ucyate bodhisattvānām akṣayā vipaśyanā.

This is called the bodhisattvas’ imperishable expanded vision.


btsun pa śa ra dva ti’i bu, de dag ni byaṅ chub sems dpa’ rnams kyi źi gnas daṅ lhag mthoṅ mi zad pa’o.

amū, bhadanta śāradvatīputra, bodhisattvānām akṣaye śamathavipaśyane.

Those, reverend Śāradvatīputra, are called the bodhisattvas’ imperishable peaceful meditation and expanded vision.

źi gnas daṅ lhag mthoṅ mi zad pa bśad zin to.
